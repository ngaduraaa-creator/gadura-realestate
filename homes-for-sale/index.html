Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions from homebuyers in Queens, Brooklyn & Long Island.

What are the best neighborhoods to buy a home in Queens?
Top neighborhoods in Queens for homebuyers include Forest Hills, Bayside, Fresh Meadows, Jamaica Estates, Howard Beach, Ozone Park, Richmond Hill, Jackson Heights, Flushing, and Whitestone. Each offers different price points, commute options, and community vibes. Nitin Gadura can help you find the right fit for your family and budget.
What is the median home price in Queens NY?
As of early 2026, the median home sale price in Queens is approximately $650,000. Prices vary significantly by neighborhood — from the high $400s in areas like Jamaica to over $1M in Forest Hills Gardens and Douglaston. Brooklyn's median is around $750,000 and Long Island's median hovers near $550,000.
How long does it take to buy a home in New York?
The typical home purchase process in New York takes 60–90 days from accepted offer to closing. This includes attorney review, mortgage commitment, title search, co-op or condo board approval (if applicable), and scheduling closing. Nitin Gadura guides buyers through every step to ensure a smooth, on-time closing.
Do I need a buyer's agent to buy a home in Queens or Brooklyn?
While not legally required, working with a buyer's agent is strongly recommended. In New York, the seller typically pays the buyer's agent commission, meaning buyer representation is free to you. Nitin Gadura provides expert buyer representation at no cost — you get full-service guidance without paying out of pocket.
What types of homes are available in Queens NY?
Queens offers a wide variety of housing types including single-family detached homes, semi-detached homes, attached townhouses, two-family and multi-family homes, co-ops, condominiums, and new construction. Multi-family homes are especially popular among buyers looking to generate rental income while building equity.
