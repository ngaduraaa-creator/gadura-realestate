Common Questions

FAQ — Buying a Home in Queens

What is the average home price in Queens NY?

Home prices in Queens vary significantly by neighborhood. In Ozone Park and Richmond Hill, single-family homes typically range from $540,000 to $720,000. Jamaica and South Ozone Park tend to run $500,000–$680,000. More affluent areas like Jamaica Estates and Kew Gardens range from $750,000 to $995,000 and above. Multi-family properties across Queens average $850,000–$1.3M depending on unit count and condition. Call us for a neighborhood-specific breakdown.

How do I start searching for homes in Queens?

Start by getting pre-approved for a mortgage so you know your true budget — this is essential in Queens' competitive market. Next, choose a local buyer's agent who knows the neighborhood-level differences across Queens. Then define your priority neighborhoods based on commute, schools, and lifestyle. From there, your agent will set up personalized MLS alerts so you see every new listing the moment it hits the market.

Does Gadura Real Estate have MLS access?

Yes. Gadura Real Estate LLC is a member of OneKey® MLS, the largest MLS in New York. Every listing we submit appears on Zillow, Realtor.com, StreetEasy, Homes.com, and hundreds of other syndication partners. As a buyer, working with us gives you access to the full MLS — not just public-facing portals — including coming-soon listings and off-market properties in our network.

How long does it take to buy a home in Queens?

From the start of your active home search to getting your keys, plan on 3–6 months in Queens. The search phase typically takes 4–8 weeks depending on market inventory in your target area. Once you have an accepted offer, the contract and due diligence period runs 4–6 weeks, followed by the mortgage commitment and closing process which takes another 30–45 days. Getting pre-approved before you start searching shortens the timeline significantly.
