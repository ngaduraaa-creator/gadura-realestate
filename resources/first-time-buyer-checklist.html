First Time Home Buyer Checklist: Queens, NY 2026

By Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Updated April 2026

Buying your first home in Queens, New York is a thrilling milestone — and one of the smartest financial decisions you can make. Queens offers incredible value, diverse neighborhoods, and strong long-term appreciation. But the process can feel overwhelming without the right guide. I'm Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate, LLC, and I've helped hundreds of first-time buyers successfully close on Queens homes. This comprehensive checklist will walk you through every step.

Phase 1: Financial Preparation

1. Check and Improve Your Credit Score

Your credit score is the foundation of your mortgage application. Pull your free credit reports from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) at AnnualCreditReport.com. For the best mortgage rates in Queens, aim for:

  • 760+: Excellent — qualifies for the best conventional rates
  • 680-759: Good — solid conventional mortgage options
  • 620-679: Fair — conventional loans available, higher rates
  • 580-619: Minimum for most FHA loans with 3.5% down

If your score needs work, pay down credit card balances below 30% utilization, dispute any errors, and avoid opening new credit accounts 6 months before applying.

2. Calculate How Much Home You Can Afford

A general rule is that your total housing costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance) should not exceed 28-31% of your gross monthly income. Use our Queens Mortgage Calculator to estimate monthly payments. Don't forget to factor in:

  • Monthly maintenance fees (co-ops) or HOA/common charges (condos)
  • Property taxes (varies significantly by Queens neighborhood)
  • Homeowner's insurance
  • Utilities and maintenance reserves

3. Save for Down Payment and Closing Costs

Here's what you'll need to have ready for a Queens home purchase:

  • Down payment: 3% (some conventional), 3.5% (FHA), 20% (most co-ops)
  • Closing costs: Typically 2-5% of purchase price (see our Closing Costs Guide)
  • Earnest money deposit: Usually 10% of purchase price in NYC
  • Moving expenses: Budget $1,000-$5,000 depending on distance
  • Emergency reserves: 3-6 months of housing costs recommended

4. Explore First-Time Buyer Assistance Programs

New York State and New York City offer excellent programs for first-time buyers:

  • SONYMA (State of New York Mortgage Agency): Below-market interest rates and down payment assistance
  • HomeFirst Down Payment Assistance: Up to $100,000 for eligible NYC buyers earning up to 80% AMI
  • FHLBNY AHP: Federal Home Loan Bank down payment grants
  • FHA 203(k) Renovation Loans: Finance a fixer-upper with one loan
  • STAR Property Tax Exemption: Reduces school tax for primary residences

Phase 2: Getting Pre-Approved

5. Gather Your Financial Documents

Before approaching a lender, assemble these documents:

  • Last 2 years of W-2s and federal tax returns
  • Recent 2-3 months of bank statements (all accounts)
  • Recent pay stubs (last 30 days)
  • Photo ID and Social Security number
  • Rental history and landlord contact (if applicable)
  • Investment account statements
  • Gift letters if using gifted funds for down payment

6. Shop Multiple Lenders

Get pre-approval quotes from at least 2-3 lenders — compare interest rates, points, origination fees, and closing costs. A 0.25% rate difference on a $500,000 Queens home saves over $25,000 over a 30-year loan. Consider local Queens banks, credit unions, and mortgage brokers alongside national lenders.

Phase 3: Finding Your Queens Home

7. Partner with a Local Queens Real Estate Agent

This is arguably the most important step. A knowledgeable local agent like Nitin Gadura understands Queens neighborhoods at a micro level — which blocks flood, where school catchment boundaries fall, which co-op boards are strict, and which listings are overpriced. Best of all, as a buyer, my services cost you nothing — the seller pays agent commissions.

8. Define Your Home Search Criteria

Be clear about your priorities before touring homes:

  • Property type: Co-op, condo, single-family, or multi-family?
  • Target neighborhoods (consider commute, schools, lifestyle)
  • Minimum bedrooms and bathrooms
  • Outdoor space requirements
  • Pet-friendly building (if applicable)
  • Must-have features vs. nice-to-haves
  • Maximum monthly carrying cost (not just purchase price)

9. Tour Properties Strategically

Visit homes at different times of day to assess traffic, noise, and natural light. Check cell service, water pressure, and storage. Look for signs of water damage, deferred maintenance, or structural issues. In co-ops, also review the building's financials and board meeting minutes.

Phase 4: Making an Offer

10. Analyze Comparable Sales (Comps)

I will pull recent sales data for similar properties in the same neighborhood to determine fair market value. In competitive Queens markets like Astoria, Bayside, and Forest Hills, well-priced homes receive multiple offers within days.

11. Craft Your Offer

Your offer strategy should include:

  • Offer price (based on comps and market conditions)
  • Contingencies: inspection, financing, appraisal
  • Earnest money deposit amount
  • Proposed closing date
  • Any seller concessions requested
  • Personal letter to sellers (optional, but sometimes effective)

12. Negotiate Effectively

Once the seller responds, I will guide you through counteroffers. In Queens, buyers often have room to negotiate 1-3% below asking price in slower markets, while competitive markets may require offering at or above asking price.

Phase 5: Due Diligence and Closing

13. Hire a Real Estate Attorney

New York State requires a real estate attorney for all property transactions. Your attorney reviews contracts, conducts title searches, and represents you at closing. Budget $1,500-$2,500 for attorney fees.

14. Order a Home Inspection

Always get a professional home inspection ($400-$700 for a Queens home). This reveals any structural, mechanical, or safety issues before you're legally committed. For co-ops, you may have limited inspection access but still review available areas.

15. Finalize Your Mortgage

Once under contract, work with your lender to complete the full mortgage application. Respond quickly to all requests for additional documentation. The bank will order an appraisal to confirm the home's value supports the loan amount.

16. Review Your Closing Disclosure

At least 3 business days before closing, you'll receive a Closing Disclosure itemizing all costs. Review it carefully against your Loan Estimate. Contact Nitin immediately if anything looks different from what was agreed.

17. Do a Final Walkthrough

Within 24-48 hours of closing, conduct a final walkthrough to verify the property is in the agreed condition, any negotiated repairs were completed, and the sellers have vacated.

18. Close on Your New Queens Home!

Bring a cashier's check or arrange a wire transfer for the remaining down payment and closing costs. Sign all documents, receive your keys, and celebrate — you're a Queens homeowner!

Queens-Specific Considerations for First-Time Buyers

Queens has unique characteristics that differ from other real estate markets:

  • Co-op Board Approval: Allow 60-90 days for the full co-op purchase and board approval process
  • Flood Zones: Many Queens neighborhoods (Howard Beach, Broad Channel, Rockaway) require flood insurance
  • NYC Transfer Tax: Buyers pay a mansion tax on purchases over $1M; sellers pay transfer tax
  • Mortgage Recording Tax: Unique to New York, this adds approximately 1.8% for loans under $500K and 1.925% for larger loans
  • Multiple Dwelling Units: Queens has many 2-4 family homes — great for owner-occupants who want rental income

Frequently Asked Questions: First Time Home Buyers in Queens, NY

How much do I need to put down as a first time buyer in Queens?

First time buyers in Queens can put as little as 3% down on certain conventional loans or 3.5% down with an FHA loan. However, most Queens co-ops require a minimum 20% down payment. SONYMA programs offer competitive low down payment options. Your total cash needed will also include closing costs and reserves.

Should I buy a co-op or condo as a first-time buyer in Queens?

Co-ops are generally more affordable but have stricter board requirements, higher down payment minimums, and monthly maintenance fees that include a share of the building's underlying mortgage. Condos offer more ownership flexibility and easier financing but typically cost more per square foot. Single-family and multi-family homes offer the most ownership control. I can help you evaluate the best fit for your situation.

How long does the home buying process take in Queens?

From accepted offer to closing, expect 60-90 days for a condo or house, and 90-120 days for a co-op (due to board approval). Getting pre-approved before you start searching keeps you ready to move quickly when you find the right home.

Do I need a real estate attorney in Queens?

Yes. New York State law effectively requires buyers to have an attorney in real estate transactions. Your attorney will review the purchase contract, conduct title searches, and represent you at the closing table. Gadura Real Estate can refer you to trusted Queens real estate attorneys.
