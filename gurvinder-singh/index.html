Gary’s Process

How Gary Works — The Listening First Philosophy

Gary’s approach to every transaction starts not with listings, but with understanding. Here is exactly what working with Gary looks like from first conversation to long after closing.

  • 1

    The Discovery Conversation

    Gary’s first call is entirely about you. Not about what homes are available — about what you’re truly looking for. Your family situation, your commute needs, your financial picture, your timeline, your cultural priorities. This conversation typically runs 45 minutes to an hour, and Gary never rushes it. By the end, Gary knows more about what you need than most agents learn in three viewings.

  • 2

    Curated, Not Overwhelming

    Gary won’t flood your inbox with 40 listings. Based on your discovery conversation, he curates a focused shortlist of properties that genuinely match your criteria. His clients routinely find their home within the first or second round of showings because the work happens before the search, not during it.

  • 3

    Walking the Neighborhood With You

    When Gary shows you a home, he shows you the block, the commute, the walk to the nearest LIRR stop or subway entrance, the closest grocery store, the school a few blocks away. He’s been in these neighborhoods for years and the contextual knowledge he offers alongside each property is something no online listing can replicate.

  • 4

    Negotiation With Backbone

    Gary is not a passive negotiator. He advises on the right offer strategy based on real market data, advocates firmly on your behalf, and knows when to push and when to hold. His clients don’t overpay and they don’t miss deals because of hesitation — Gary moves decisively while keeping you fully informed.

  • 5

    Closing and Beyond

    At closing, Gary is not checking a box. He’s adding you to a client relationship he intends to maintain for years. Post-closing, he stays available for contractor referrals, renovation second opinions, refinancing timing conversations, and honest market updates. If you ever want to sell or upgrade, he’s the first call you make — and he’s already tracking the market for you.

After the Keys

The Post-Closing Relationship — Why Gary’s Clients Call Him Years Later

The closing table doesn’t end Gary’s job — it begins a different phase of it. Here’s what Gary’s past clients actually call him about, years after they got their keys:

  • Recommendations for reliable local contractors (plumbers, electricians, roofers) who Gary has personally vetted over years of use
  • Second opinions before starting major renovation projects — is the scope realistic? Is the timeline right? Is the cost worth it for the neighborhood?
  • Advice on whether the current market timing is right to refinance or pull equity
  • Guidance on whether to sell, rent out a unit, or hold for another few years given current conditions
  • Referrals to trusted attorneys for estate planning, title transfers, or family property situations
  • Simple check-ins on neighborhood market trends — Gary proactively shares updates with longtime clients
  • Help when adult children are ready to buy their own home — Gary becomes the family’s agent across generations
