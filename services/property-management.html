Owning rental property in the New York City metro area can be incredibly rewarding — but managing tenants, maintenance, compliance, and rent collection takes significant time and expertise. Our Property Management Services

  • Tenant Screening & Placement: Thorough background checks including credit history, employment verification, income documentation, landlord references, and eviction history. We find qualified, reliable tenants who pay on time and take care of your property.
  • Rent Collection & Financial Reporting: Consistent, on-time rent collection with direct deposit to your account. Monthly financial statements detail all income and expenses so you have complete visibility into your property's performance.
  • Maintenance & Repairs: 24/7 maintenance coordination through our network of licensed, insured contractors. From emergency plumbing to routine HVAC servicing, we handle all repair requests promptly and cost-effectively.
  • Lease Preparation & Renewals: Legally compliant lease agreements tailored to New York law, including proper riders and disclosures. We manage lease renewals, rent increase notices, and lease terminations in full compliance with local regulations.
  • Property Inspections: Regular interior and exterior inspections to identify maintenance issues before they become expensive problems. Move-in and move-out inspections protect your property and security deposit interests.
  • Regulatory Compliance: We ensure your property meets all NYC Department of Buildings requirements, HPD registration obligations, lead paint disclosure rules, and local housing codes.
  • Vacancy Marketing: Professional listing photos, targeted advertising on major rental platforms, and rapid tenant placement to minimize vacancy periods and lost income.

Why New York Landlords Need Professional Management

New York has some of the most tenant-protective laws in the country. Self-managing landlords face significant legal risks if they are not current on regulations including:

  • Rent Stabilization: If your building qualifies, strict rules govern allowable rent increases, lease renewal terms, and tenant protections. Violations carry steep penalties.
  • Housing Maintenance Code: NYC holds landlords to rigorous standards for heat, hot water, pest control, and building maintenance. HPD violations can result in fines and legal action.
  • Lead Paint Laws: Properties built before 1978 have extensive disclosure and remediation requirements, especially with tenants who have children under six.
  • Eviction Procedures: New York's eviction process is court-supervised and time-consuming. Procedural errors can delay evictions by months and expose landlords to liability.
  • Security Deposit Rules: Recent changes require landlords to hold deposits in interest-bearing accounts and limit amounts to one month's rent for most residential tenants.
