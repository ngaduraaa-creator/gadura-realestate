Long Island Commercial Real Estate

Long Island's commercial market serves both the island's 2.8 million residents and overflow demand from New York City. Key Long Island commercial segments include:

  • Nassau County retail corridors: Hempstead Turnpike, Merrick Road, Old Country Road, and Stewart Avenue are major retail strips with strong suburban traffic counts
  • Garden City and Mineola office: Nassau County's primary office markets attracting professional services, medical offices, and financial tenants
  • Bethpage / Hauppauge industrial corridor: Suffolk County's industrial spine, serving manufacturing, logistics, and technology tenants
  • Hamptons commercial: High-end retail and restaurant space in Southampton, East Hampton, and Sag Harbor commands premium seasonal and year-round rents

Commercial Real Estate Due Diligence in Queens

Commercial transactions require more extensive due diligence than residential. Key items I help clients navigate include:

  • Zoning verification: Confirm the intended use is permitted under current zoning and obtain copies of any variances
  • Environmental Phase I assessment: Required by most commercial lenders; identifies potential environmental contamination
  • Lease review: All existing tenant leases must be reviewed by a commercial real estate attorney for terms, options, and restrictions
  • Certificate of Occupancy: Verify the CO matches current use and all prior work was properly permitted
  • Income and expense verification: Review actual rent rolls, operating expenses, and tax bills — not just seller projections
  • Title search: Commercial title searches are more complex and must check for easements, liens, and restrictive covenants
