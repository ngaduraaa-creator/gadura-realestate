Luxury Real Estate Markets: Queens & Long Island

Luxury Queens: Where Premium Meets Convenience

Queens' luxury market spans a wide range of property types and neighborhoods, each offering a distinct lifestyle premium:

Long Island City

The epicenter of Queens luxury condo living. Waterfront towers and modern high-rises with direct Manhattan skyline views, concierge services, rooftop pools, and world-class amenities. LIC luxury condos represent some of the best value in NYC for price-per-square-foot vs. Manhattan.

$900K – $3M+

Bayside & Douglaston

Northeastern Queens' most prestigious neighborhoods. Spacious single-family homes with manicured properties, mature trees, and an affluent suburban character. Top-rated school districts, golf courses, and the Little Neck Bay waterfront make this one of Queens' most desirable enclaves.

$900K – $2.5M+

Jamaica Estates

Queens' historic luxury neighborhood, featuring large Tudor and Colonial homes on expansive lots. Home to business leaders, professionals, and diplomats. Jamaica Estates' tree-lined streets and distinctive gateposts create an enclave feel within the larger borough.

$800K – $2M+

Forest Hills Gardens

One of the most architecturally distinctive neighborhoods in all of New York City. This planned community features English-style homes, cobblestone streets, and a private park — all with excellent LIRR and subway access. Forest Hills Gardens homes rarely come to market and command significant premiums.

$1.2M – $3M+

Luxury Long Island: Space, Prestige, and Natural Beauty

Long Island's luxury market is anchored by its prestigious North Shore communities, Gold Coast estates, and the world-famous Hamptons. For buyers seeking more space, larger properties, and a different pace of life, Long Island's luxury offerings are unparalleled in the New York metro area.

Manhasset & Flower Hill

Nassau County's most coveted address. World-class Manhasset school district, "Miracle Mile" luxury shopping, and magnificent homes on large lots. Consistently ranked among the top communities in all of New York State.

$1.5M – $5M+

Garden City

Beautifully planned village with tree-lined streets, stunning architecture, top schools, and a charming walkable downtown. Garden City offers the quintessential Long Island luxury lifestyle with convenient LIRR access to Penn Station.

$1.2M – $4M+

Great Neck

The inspiration for F. Scott Fitzgerald's West Egg, Great Neck remains one of Long Island's most prestigious and diverse communities. Waterfront estates, top-ranked schools, and 15-minute express LIRR service to Penn Station.

$1M – $5M+

Old Westbury & Brookville

Long Island's most exclusive equestrian and estate communities. Sprawling properties of 2-10+ acres with historic manor homes, horse facilities, and unrivaled privacy. Old Westbury and Brookville are where true privacy and prestige meet.

$2M – $15M+

Luxury Buyer's Guide: What to Know Before Purchasing a Queens or Long Island Luxury Home

  • Mansion Tax: All NYC residential purchases of $1M+ trigger the mansion tax. Budget accordingly — see our Closing Costs Guide for full detail
  • Jumbo Financing: Most luxury Queens and LI purchases require jumbo loans. Requirements are stricter and down payments are typically 20-30%. Work with a lender who specializes in jumbo products
  • Due Diligence: Luxury properties warrant expanded inspections — structural engineers, oil tank surveys (common on Long Island), environmental assessments, and pool/spa inspections all add important protection
  • Property Taxes: Long Island property taxes for luxury homes can run $20,000-$60,000+ annually. Understand your tax obligation before committing to a purchase
  • HOA / Building Financials: For luxury condos, review the building's reserve fund, pending special assessments, and litigation history carefully
