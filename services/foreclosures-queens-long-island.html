Types of Distressed Properties Available

  • Pre-Foreclosures: Properties where the owner is behind on mortgage payments. Often available at below-market prices through short sales.
  • Bank-Owned (REO) Properties: Homes that have been repossessed by the lender after a failed auction. Banks are often motivated sellers.
  • Auction Properties: Homes sold at public auction, typically at the courthouse. Can offer deep discounts but require cash and carry higher risk.
  • HUD Homes: Properties insured by FHA that went into foreclosure. Available through HUD's official sales process with special programs for owner-occupants.

Important Considerations for Foreclosure Buyers

Buying a foreclosure is different from a traditional purchase. Here's what you need to know:

  • Most foreclosures are sold "as-is" — inspections are critical but repairs are your responsibility
  • Bank timelines are often longer than traditional sales — patience is required
  • Title issues are more common — always get title insurance
  • Competition from investors is real — having an experienced agent gives you an edge
  • Renovation financing options exist — FHA 203(k) and Fannie Mae HomeStyle loans bundle purchase and repair costs
