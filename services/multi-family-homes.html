Types of Multi-Family Properties We Specialize In

  • Two-Family Homes: The most common type in Queens and Brooklyn. Live in one unit, rent the other to offset your mortgage. Popular in Ozone Park, Richmond Hill, Woodhaven, and Canarsie.
  • Three-Family Homes: Maximum rental income with one owner-occupied unit. Found throughout Jamaica, East Flatbush, and Flatbush.
  • Four-Family Properties: The largest you can buy with residential financing. Available across southeast Queens and eastern Brooklyn.
  • Mixed-Use Buildings: Commercial ground floor with residential units above. Found along major corridors in Jackson Heights, Corona, and Bushwick.

Benefits of Multi-Family Homeownership

  • Rental income: Tenants help pay your mortgage, reducing your monthly housing cost significantly
  • FHA financing available: Buy with as little as 3.5% down for owner-occupied 2-4 unit properties
  • Multi-generational living: Separate units for parents, children, and extended family while maintaining privacy
  • Wealth building: Property appreciation plus rental income accelerates your path to financial freedom
  • Tax advantages: Deduct expenses related to rental units including maintenance, insurance, and depreciation

Best Neighborhoods for Multi-Family Homes

Queens and Brooklyn offer excellent multi-family housing stock in established neighborhoods with strong rental demand. The most popular areas include:

  • Richmond Hill — Guyanese community hub with plentiful 2-3 family homes
  • Ozone Park & South Ozone Park — Indian and Guyanese families, affordable multi-family stock
  • Jamaica — Transit hub, diverse community, strong rental demand
  • East Flatbush & Flatbush — Caribbean community, affordable entry points
  • Canarsie — Family neighborhood, Caribbean community, detached multi-family homes
  • Ridgewood — Queens/Brooklyn border, growing demand, multi-family housing
