Buy a Brand-New Home With Expert Representation

Purchasing a newly constructed home is one of the most exciting decisions a buyer can make — but the process differs significantly from buying a resale property. From negotiating directly with builders to understanding construction timelines and warranty protections, new construction transactions demand specialized knowledge. I'm Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate, LLC, and I guide buyers through every stage of the new construction process across Queens, Brooklyn, and Long Island.

New development is booming across our service area. Queens neighborhoods such as Long Island City, Jamaica, and Flushing are seeing new condo towers and townhouse developments. Brooklyn communities from Bushwick to East New York are experiencing a wave of new multi-family and mixed-use construction. On Long Island, builders are delivering single-family subdivisions, 55+ active-adult communities, and luxury custom homes across Nassau and Suffolk Counties.

Types of New Construction We Handle

  • Pre-Construction Condos: Purchase early in the development timeline, often at discounted pricing, before the building is completed. We review offering plans, sponsor terms, and projected closing timelines.
  • New Townhouses & Row Houses: Modern attached homes being built throughout Queens and Brooklyn, offering open layouts, private outdoor space, and energy-efficient systems.
  • Single-Family New Builds: Custom and production homes on Long Island, from entry-level starter homes to luxury estates with every modern amenity.
  • 55+ Active Adult Communities: Age-restricted developments on Long Island with maintenance-free living, clubhouses, and resort-style amenities.
  • Mixed-Use Developments: Ground-floor commercial with residential above — ideal for investors seeking rental income from day one.

Why You Need a Buyer's Agent for New Construction

Many buyers mistakenly believe they can handle a new construction purchase without their own agent. The builder's sales team represents the builder — not you. Having Nitin Gadura as your buyer's agent provides critical advantages:

  • Price negotiation: Builders have margin on upgrades, closing cost credits, and incentive packages. An experienced agent knows where the negotiation room exists.
  • Contract review: Builder contracts are heavily weighted in the developer's favor. Your agent ensures your interests are protected with proper contingencies and deadlines.
  • Construction monitoring: We conduct pre-drywall inspections, final walk-throughs, and punch-list reviews to ensure the builder delivers what was promised.
  • Upgrade guidance: Not all upgrades add value. Nitin advises which selections maximize your home's long-term worth and which are unnecessary markups.
  • Timeline management: Construction delays are common. Your agent holds the builder accountable to agreed-upon completion dates and milestone benchmarks.

New Construction Hot Spots in 2026

Several areas are experiencing significant new development activity:

  • Long Island City, Queens: Continued high-rise condo development along the waterfront with Manhattan skyline views and direct subway access.
  • Jamaica, Queens: Major redevelopment zone with new mixed-use towers, affordable housing, and transit-oriented development near the AirTrain hub.
  • East New York, Brooklyn: Rezoning has unlocked thousands of new housing units, with both affordable and market-rate developments underway.
  • Hempstead & Elmont, Long Island: New subdivision construction near the Belmont Park redevelopment and UBS Arena, bringing fresh inventory to one of Nassau County's most accessible communities.
  • Babylon & Islip, Long Island: New single-family and townhouse communities offering modern amenities at more accessible Long Island price points.

The New Construction Buying Process

Here is what to expect when purchasing new construction with Nitin Gadura:

  • Step 1: Pre-qualification with a lender experienced in new construction financing
  • Step 2: Tour active developments, model homes, and review floor plans
  • Step 3: Submit a reservation or purchase agreement with earnest money
  • Step 4: Select finishes, upgrades, and customization options
  • Step 5: Pre-drywall inspection and construction progress updates
  • Step 6: Final walk-through, punch list review, and closing
