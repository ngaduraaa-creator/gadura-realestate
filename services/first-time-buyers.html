Down Payment Assistance & First-Time Buyer Programs

One of the biggest hurdles for first-time buyers is the down payment. The good news is that multiple federal, state, and local programs exist to help:

  • SONYMA (State of New York Mortgage Agency): Offers below-market interest rates, low down payments (as little as 3%), and down payment assistance grants for qualifying first-time buyers throughout New York State.
  • FHA Loans: Federal Housing Administration loans require as little as 3.5% down with more flexible credit requirements than conventional loans. Popular for Queens and Brooklyn purchases.
  • HomeFirst Down Payment Assistance (NYC HPD): Up to $100,000 in down payment assistance for income-eligible first-time buyers purchasing in New York City, including Queens and Brooklyn.
  • Conventional 97 Loans: Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac programs allowing just 3% down for qualified first-time buyers.
  • VA Loans: Zero-down-payment mortgages for eligible veterans and active-duty military personnel.
  • Nassau County First-Time Buyer Programs: County-level assistance programs for Long Island buyers, including down payment help and reduced interest rate programs.

The First-Time Buyer Process: Step by Step

Here is exactly how Nitin Gadura walks first-time buyers through the purchase process:

  • Step 1 — Financial Review: We assess your savings, income, credit, and debt to determine your true buying power and identify the best loan programs for your situation.
  • Step 2 — Pre-Approval: I connect you with trusted lenders who specialize in first-time buyer programs. You will know exactly how much home you can afford before we start looking.
  • Step 3 — Neighborhood Selection: Based on your budget, commute, lifestyle, and priorities, we narrow down the best neighborhoods. My neighborhood guides help you compare options.
  • Step 4 — House Hunting: We tour homes together, and I point out what first-time buyers often miss — structural issues, renovation costs, hidden expenses, and resale potential.

  • Step 6 — Inspection & Due Diligence: We schedule a thorough home inspection, review the results, and negotiate repairs or credits if needed.
  • Step 7 — Closing: I coordinate with your attorney, lender, and title company to ensure a smooth closing. You will understand every dollar on the closing disclosure before you sign. See our Closing Costs Guide for details.

Best Neighborhoods for First-Time Buyers

Affordability and value vary dramatically across our service area. These neighborhoods consistently offer the best opportunities for first-time buyers in 2026:

  • Jamaica, Queens: Median prices below the Queens average, excellent transit (E/J/Z, LIRR, AirTrain), and active redevelopment bringing new amenities.
  • Ozone Park, Queens: Affordable single and two-family homes with strong community character and easy access to JFK Airport and the A train.
  • Richmond Hill, Queens: Charming Victorian-era homes and two-family properties at prices well below the borough median.
  • East New York, Brooklyn: One of Brooklyn's most affordable neighborhoods with new construction and improving infrastructure.
  • Elmont & Valley Stream, Long Island: Entry-level Nassau County communities with strong schools and quick LIRR access to Penn Station.

Common First-Time Buyer Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not getting pre-approved before touring homes — you may fall in love with a home you cannot afford
  • Underestimating closing costs — in NYC, expect 3-6% of the purchase price on top of your down payment
  • Skipping the home inspection to save money — this can cost thousands in unforeseen repairs
  • Making large purchases or changing jobs during the mortgage process — this can derail your loan approval
  • Not using a buyer's agent — my services cost you nothing as a buyer, yet I save you time, money, and stress
