First-Time Home Buyer's Guide to Long Island

By Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Updated April 2026

Long Island is where many New Yorkers make the leap from renting in the city to owning a home with a yard, a good school district, and a quieter pace of life. But the process of buying on Long Island is different from buying in NYC, and first-time buyers face unique challenges. I'm Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate, and I guide first-time buyers through every step of the Long Island home purchase process.

Step 1: Understand What You Can Afford

Long Island home prices vary dramatically by town and county. Here is a general overview for 2026:

  • Western Nassau County (Elmont, Valley Stream, Hempstead): $450,000 - $650,000
  • Central Nassau County (Levittown, Hicksville, Bethpage): $500,000 - $700,000
  • North Shore Nassau (Great Neck, Manhasset, Roslyn): $800,000 - $2M+
  • South Shore Nassau (Merrick, Bellmore, Massapequa): $550,000 - $800,000
  • Western Suffolk (Lindenhurst, Babylon, West Islip): $450,000 - $650,000

Beyond the purchase price, Long Island property taxes are among the highest in the nation. Budget $8,000 to $20,000+ annually depending on the town. Nitin Gadura always helps buyers factor in the full cost of ownership, not just the mortgage payment.

Step 2: Get Pre-Approved for a Mortgage

Long Island is a competitive market, and sellers want to see a pre-approval letter with every offer. Here are the main mortgage options for first-time buyers:

  • Conventional loan: 5-20% down payment. Best rates with 20% down and 740+ credit score. No PMI with 20% down
  • FHA loan: 3.5% down payment with a 580+ credit score. Popular with first-time buyers. Mortgage insurance required
  • SONYMA (State of New York Mortgage Agency): Low down payment programs specifically for New York first-time buyers with income limits. Competitive rates and down payment assistance available
  • VA loan: 0% down for eligible veterans and active-duty military. No PMI
  • USDA loan: 0% down for eligible properties in qualifying rural areas of eastern Suffolk County

Step 3: Choose the Right Town

The town you choose determines your school district, property taxes, and commute. For first-time buyers on a budget, Gadura Real Estate recommends these affordable starter towns:

Best Affordable Nassau County Towns

  • Elmont: Right at the Queens border. Homes from $450,000. Belmont Park and the new UBS Arena nearby. Short drive to Queens amenities
  • Valley Stream: LIRR station with 35-minute Penn Station commute. Homes from $475,000. Green Acres Mall for shopping
  • Uniondale: Centrally located with homes from $400,000. Near Hofstra University and Nassau Coliseum
  • Hempstead: Some of the most affordable single-family homes in Nassau County starting around $350,000

Best Affordable Suffolk County Towns

  • Lindenhurst: South Shore village with homes from $400,000. LIRR access. Near water
  • Central Islip: Affordable homes from $350,000. Close to courthouses and county government employers
  • Brentwood: Large community with homes starting around $375,000. Growing commercial district

Step 4: Understand Long Island Property Taxes

This is the single biggest surprise for first-time buyers coming from NYC. Long Island property taxes fund local school districts, village services, and county government. Key points:

  • Taxes are assessed annually and typically paid in two installments
  • The STAR exemption provides a property tax reduction for owner-occupied primary residences
  • Enhanced STAR is available for seniors 65+ with income under $98,700
  • Tax grievance filings can reduce your assessment if your home is over-assessed
  • Nitin Gadura can estimate the total monthly cost (mortgage + taxes + insurance) for any property before you make an offer

Step 5: The Offer and Contract Process

Long Island follows a different process than NYC co-ops and condos:

  • Make an offer: Your agent submits an offer to the seller's agent. Negotiation follows
  • Accepted offer: Once agreed, both sides hire attorneys (required in New York State)
  • Home inspection: A professional inspection typically costs $500-$800 and is essential. Results may lead to further negotiation
  • Attorney review: Your attorney reviews and negotiates the contract of sale
  • Contract signing: You sign the contract and provide a 10% deposit (held in escrow)
  • Mortgage commitment: Your lender completes underwriting and issues a commitment letter
  • Closing: Title search, final walkthrough, and closing at the attorney's office. You receive the keys

The entire process typically takes 60-90 days from accepted offer to closing.

Common First-Time Buyer Mistakes on Long Island

  • Ignoring property taxes: A $500,000 home with $15,000 annual taxes costs more monthly than a $600,000 home with $8,000 taxes
  • Skipping the home inspection: Never waive the inspection, especially on older Long Island homes that may have hidden issues
  • Not researching flood zones: Some South Shore areas require flood insurance, adding $1,000-$5,000+ annually
  • Choosing a town only for price: The cheapest town may not have the best schools or commute for your situation
