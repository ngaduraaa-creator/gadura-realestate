VA Home Loan Guide for Queens & Long Island Veterans (2026)

By Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Published June 2026

If you are a veteran or active-duty service member looking to buy a home in Queens or Long Island, the VA home loan is the single most powerful financing tool available to you. Zero down payment, no private mortgage insurance, competitive interest rates, and no loan limit for veterans with full entitlement. No other mortgage program in America comes close.

I am Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate, and I work with veterans across Queens and Long Island to find homes that qualify for VA financing, navigate the VA appraisal process, and combine VA loan benefits with New York's veteran property tax exemptions to create the most affordable possible homeownership scenario.

VA Loan Benefits: Why It Is the Best Mortgage in America

Zero Down Payment

The most significant advantage of a VA loan is the ability to finance 100% of the purchase price. While conventional loans require 5-20% down and FHA loans require 3.5% down, VA loans require nothing. On a $750,000 Queens home, that is $26,250 (FHA) to $150,000 (conventional 20%) that stays in your bank account.

No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

Conventional loans with less than 20% down require PMI, which adds $200 to $400 per month on a typical Queens purchase. FHA loans require mortgage insurance for the life of the loan. VA loans have zero PMI, ever. Over a 30-year loan, this saves $72,000 to $144,000 compared to FHA.

Competitive Interest Rates

VA loan interest rates are typically 0.25% to 0.50% lower than conventional rates because the VA guaranty reduces lender risk. On a $750,000 loan, a 0.25% rate difference saves approximately $40,000 over 30 years.

No Loan Limit for Full Entitlement

Veterans with full entitlement (meaning they have never used their VA loan benefit before, or have fully restored it) have no loan limit. You can finance a $1.5 million home with zero down. Veterans with partial entitlement are subject to the conforming loan limit of $1,149,825 for NYC in 2026.

VA Funding Fee

The VA funding fee is a one-time fee charged on VA loans (2.15% for first-time users with zero down, reduced for subsequent uses and for veterans making a down payment). This fee can be rolled into the loan. Veterans with service-connected disabilities are exempt from the funding fee entirely, saving $16,125 on a $750,000 loan.

VA Loan Eligibility

Who Qualifies

  • Active duty: Currently serving with at least 90 continuous days of service
  • Veterans: Honorably discharged with minimum service requirements (90 days wartime, 181 days peacetime, or 6 years in the National Guard/Reserves)
  • Surviving spouses: Unremarried surviving spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability

Certificate of Eligibility (COE)

To use your VA loan benefit, you need a Certificate of Eligibility. You can obtain it through:

  • eBenefits portal (va.gov) — fastest method, often instant
  • Your VA-approved lender can pull it electronically
  • Mail VA Form 26-1880 with your DD-214

Queens Homes That Qualify for VA Loans

VA loans can be used for:

  • Single-family homes: The majority of Queens detached and semi-attached houses qualify
  • Two- to four-family homes: As long as you occupy one unit. This is ideal for house-hacking in Queens neighborhoods like Ozone Park, Jamaica, and South Ozone Park
  • VA-approved condos: The condo complex must be on the VA's approved list. Many Queens condos are approved; check the VA's searchable database
  • Co-ops: VA loans for co-ops are rare but possible in New York State. Some VA lenders in the NYC market handle co-op VA loans
  • New construction: Purchasing from a builder who participates in VA programs

VA Minimum Property Requirements

VA appraisers evaluate properties against Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs). Common issues in Queens homes that can cause problems:

  • Peeling paint on pre-1978 homes: Lead paint is a VA concern. Any peeling or chipping paint must be remediated before closing.
  • Roof condition: Must have at least 2-3 years of remaining useful life
  • Heating system: Must be functional and adequate for the climate
  • Plumbing and electrical: Must be safe and functional
  • Termite inspection: Required in the NYC/Long Island area
  • Handrails on stairs: Required on all stairways with three or more risers

An experienced Queens agent can identify potential VA appraisal issues before you make an offer, saving time and avoiding deal-killing surprises.

House-Hacking With VA Loans in Queens

The VA loan's ability to finance multi-family properties with zero down payment creates one of the most powerful wealth-building strategies available to veterans. Here is how it works in Queens:

  1. Purchase a two-family home in Queens with $0 down using your VA loan
  2. Live in one unit as your primary residence
  3. Rent the second unit at market rates
  4. Use the rental income to cover most or all of your mortgage payment

Example: Two-Family House-Hack in Jamaica, Queens

  • Purchase price: $780,000
  • Down payment: $0 (VA loan)
  • Monthly mortgage (P&I at 6.0%): $4,675
  • Property taxes: $575/month
  • Insurance: $250/month
  • Total monthly cost: $5,500
  • Rental income from second unit: $2,400/month
  • Your effective housing cost: $3,100/month

With veteran property tax exemptions applied, the effective cost drops further. A disabled veteran could potentially reduce their housing cost below $2,500 per month for a home they own, while building equity and benefiting from Queens real estate appreciation. Property Tax Exemptions for Queens Veterans

New York offers some of the most generous veteran property tax exemptions in the country. These exemptions stack with STAR for maximum savings.

Alternative Veterans Exemption

  • Basic: 15% reduction in assessed value for qualifying wartime service or expeditionary medal
  • Combat zone: Additional 10% for combat theater service
  • Disability: Additional exemption based on VA disability rating (up to full exemption for 100% disability)

Combined Savings Example

A 70% disabled veteran in Queens paying $7,000/year in property taxes who qualifies for all applicable exemptions:

  • Basic veterans exemption: -$1,050
  • Combat zone exemption: -$700
  • Disability exemption: -$1,400
  • Enhanced STAR: -$750
  • Total annual savings: approximately $3,900
  • Effective annual property taxes: approximately $3,100
