ITIN Buyers — How to Purchase a Home in NY Without an SSN

By Nitin Gadura · April 23, 2026

Owning a home in Queens, Brooklyn, or Long Island without a Social Security Number is not only possible — it happens regularly in our immigrant-rich communities. An Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN), paired with a lender that specializes in ITIN mortgages, opens the same housing market available to SSN buyers. Rates are higher, down payments larger, but the path is real. Here's how it works in 2026.

Know your rights up front. Federal Fair Housing Act, NY Human Rights Law §296, and NYC Administrative Code §8-107 prohibit discrimination based on national origin, immigration status, or citizenship. A seller, landlord, or broker may not refuse to sell, quote higher prices, or demand different terms because you are an ITIN buyer. See our Fair Housing policy for the full protected-class list.

What an ITIN Is

An ITIN is a tax processing number issued by the IRS to individuals who must file U.S. tax returns but are not eligible for a Social Security Number. ITINs have the format 9XX-XX-XXXX (the first digit is always 9) [1]. They are issued regardless of immigration status, solely for federal tax compliance purposes.

People who commonly hold ITINs include:

  • Non-resident aliens required to file US returns
  • Resident aliens ineligible for an SSN
  • Dependents or spouses of US citizens / resident aliens
  • Dependents or spouses of visa holders
  • Undocumented immigrants who file US taxes

Filing taxes annually with an ITIN builds a documented income and tax-payment history — exactly what mortgage lenders need to underwrite a loan.

What You'll Need to Qualify

Document preparation is where ITIN buyers get ahead. The stronger and cleaner your paperwork, the better your rate and down payment terms. Budget 30 days of document assembly before you start serious house hunting.

Tax history (most important)

  • At least 2 years of U.S. federal tax returns filed under your ITIN (Form 1040 with all schedules)
  • Tax returns must show consistent income that matches your bank deposits
  • Lender may request IRS tax transcripts directly — Form 4506-C authorizes this

Identity and residency documentation

  • Valid foreign passport
  • ITIN letter from the IRS confirming your number
  • Proof of U.S. residence (utility bills, lease, W-2, driver's license)
  • Employment authorization document or visa if applicable
  • Proof of stable U.S. address for 12+ months typically preferred

Income and assets

  • Most recent 2 months of bank statements (all accounts)
  • Pay stubs covering 30+ days
  • Employment verification letter
  • If self-employed: business tax returns, 1099 forms, P&L statements, CPA letter
  • Source-of-funds documentation for the down payment

Credit

Lenders pull traditional credit reports using your ITIN if any credit history exists. If you have limited or no traditional credit, many ITIN-specialist lenders will accept "non-traditional" credit evidence — rent payment history, utility bill history, insurance payment history, cell phone payment history.

The Process — Step by Step

  1. Consult a lender specializing in ITIN mortgages. Ask specifically how many ITIN deals they closed in the last 12 months. Fewer than 20 = inexperienced. A good ITIN lender will have a clear intake checklist and realistic rate/down payment quotes.
  2. Get pre-approved. Pre-approval for ITIN borrowers functions the same as for SSN borrowers — credit pull, document review, income verification. You walk out with a letter that Queens sellers accept.
  3. House hunt with confidence. Your offer carries the same weight as any other financed offer. Sellers don't see your ITIN status until contract.
  4. Retain a NY-licensed real estate attorney experienced with ITIN buyers. Contract language, closing document handling, and identity verification for the NY State and NYC RPT filings are legal work. Your attorney ensures everything aligns.
  5. Close as normal. NY transfer tax filings (Form TP-584, NYC RPTT) accept ITINs as buyer identification. Title insurance, recording, and all standard closing steps proceed identically.
  6. After closing: register for NY State STAR if you intend to use the home as your primary residence. STAR eligibility is not tied to citizenship or immigration status — see our STAR guide.

Fair-Housing Protections for ITIN Buyers

This is critical because many ITIN buyers have experienced discrimination in other contexts and may hesitate to exercise housing rights. In New York, the law is clear:

  • Federal Fair Housing Act — prohibits discrimination based on national origin [3]
  • New York Human Rights Law §296 — prohibits housing discrimination based on citizenship status [4]
  • NYC Administrative Code §8-107 — NYC's broader protections include citizenship or immigration status as explicit protected classes [5]

This means a Queens seller cannot:

  • Refuse to accept your offer because you're an ITIN buyer
  • Require a larger deposit than SSN buyers
  • Demand additional terms or concessions
  • Reject your offer without substantive reason

A Queens real estate broker (buyer's or listing) is similarly prohibited from steering you away from neighborhoods based on national origin or immigration status.

If you believe you've been discriminated against, file a complaint with:

  • U.S. Department of HUD — hud.gov/fairhousing · 1-800-669-9777
  • NY State Division of Human Rights — dhr.ny.gov · 1-888-392-3644
  • NYC Commission on Human Rights — nyc.gov/cchr · 311

Common Questions

Can I buy with a cash purchase and no ITIN?

Yes. Cash purchases don't require any tax identification beyond what's needed for the TP-584 transfer tax filing. You'll need a valid identification document (passport) and your attorney handles the rest. Many foreign nationals and non-resident buyers purchase NYC/Queens property in cash.

Do I qualify for FHA or VA loans?

FHA is generally available to non-citizens who have lawful permanent residence (green card) or non-permanent resident alien status with valid work authorization. ITIN alone without documented lawful residence typically does not qualify for FHA [6]. VA loans require US military service, so they are tied to veteran eligibility rather than citizenship.

Can I qualify for SONYMA or HomeFirst?

SONYMA and NYC HomeFirst eligibility rules vary. Most require lawful residency documentation. Non-citizens with lawful status (green card, asylum grant, certain visa categories) often qualify. ITIN alone without lawful-presence documentation generally does not. See our down payment assistance guide for specific program requirements.

Can my spouse and I use both an ITIN and an SSN on one mortgage?

Yes. Mixed-status couples commonly use one partner's SSN to secure standard financing and list the ITIN partner on title. Strategy depends on income and credit profiles — your lender evaluates both paths.

What happens if I get an SSN later?

Nothing changes on your existing home loan. When you later refinance, you can do so using your SSN and may qualify for better conventional pricing at that time.

Will buying a home affect my immigration case?

Home ownership is not a bar to any immigration proceeding. It does not create any new immigration risk. Many immigration attorneys actually cite home ownership as evidence of strong US ties during removal-defense proceedings. However, immigration law is specialized — talk to an immigration attorney about your specific situation, not a real estate broker.
