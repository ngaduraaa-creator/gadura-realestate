Down Payment Assistance Programs in New York — 2026 Guide

By Nitin Gadura · April 21, 2026

If the down payment is the only thing keeping you from buying, you may not need as much as you think. New York State and New York City both run active programs that provide grants or low-cost loans toward your down payment and closing costs. Here's the 2026 landscape — what's real, who qualifies, and how to stack programs.

The Four Programs to Know

1. SONYMA — State of New York Mortgage Agency

SONYMA is the state housing finance agency. It offers below-market mortgages paired with down payment assistance loans (DPAL) [1]. Key features:

  • Fixed-rate mortgages, typically competitive with or below conventional rates
  • Down Payment Assistance Loan: up to the greater of $15,000 or 3% of purchase price (up to $20,000 in some programs)
  • DPAL forgiven after 10 years of continuous ownership and occupancy
  • Income and purchase-price limits vary by county (Queens limits typically permit household incomes up to six figures)
  • First-time buyer typically required (some programs waive this in target areas)
  • Must complete a homebuyer education course

2. HomeFirst — NYC HPD

NYC Department of Housing Preservation & Development's HomeFirst program provides qualified first-time buyers with up to $100,000 toward down payment or closing costs on 1–4 family homes, condos, or co-ops in any of the five boroughs [2].

  • Forgivable loan — forgiven over 10–15 years depending on assistance amount
  • Must be first-time buyer or not owned a home in the last three years
  • Household income limits apply (tied to area median income)
  • Required homebuyer education through HPD-approved counselors
  • Property must be primary residence for the retention period

3. SONYMA "Achieving the Dream" & "Low Interest Rate" Programs

Two SONYMA product families worth comparing [1]:

  • Achieving the Dream: lowest income limits, lowest rates, ideal for moderate-income first-time buyers
  • Low Interest Rate: higher income limits, still below-market rate, broader eligibility

4. Neighborhood-Specific & CDFI Programs

  • Neighborhood Housing Services of Queens / NYC — counseling and occasional funds
  • Chhaya CDC — South Asian & Indo-Caribbean community-focused housing counseling [3]
  • Parodneck Foundation — closing-cost assistance for select NYC buyers
  • Habitat for Humanity NYC — ownership opportunities via sweat-equity model

Stacking Programs

Many NY buyers successfully stack SONYMA + HomeFirst in a single closing. The SONYMA first-mortgage sits in lien position 1; HomeFirst and DPAL sit as second/third liens that are forgiven over time. Your lender must be approved for both programs — not every lender is.

Real Numbers Example (Queens Two-Family, 2026)

Purchase price: $850,000. Buyer's liquid savings: $40,000.

  • FHA 3.5% down: $29,750
  • Closing costs (buyer side, NYC): ~$30,000
  • HomeFirst assistance: up to $100,000 (far more than needed here) — closes the gap
  • SONYMA DPAL: $15,000–$20,000 supplementing down payment if not using HomeFirst
  • Buyer's out-of-pocket after stacking: potentially under $15,000

This is directional, not a guarantee. Actual eligibility depends on income, credit, property type, and occupancy intent.

How to Start

  1. Complete a HUD-approved homebuyer education course — required by most programs [4]
  2. Get pre-approved with a SONYMA- and HomeFirst-participating lender
  3. Pull your current credit — minimum scores typically 640 for SONYMA, 620 for FHA
  4. Gather 2 years of tax returns, 2 months of bank statements, 30 days of pay stubs
  5. Call me — I coordinate with lenders who know both programs
