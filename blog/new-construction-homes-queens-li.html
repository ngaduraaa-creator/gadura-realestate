New Construction Opportunities in Queens and Long Island

New construction homes offer buyers the chance to customize their living space, enjoy modern amenities, and avoid the renovation costs that come with older properties. Queens and Long Island have seen significant new development activity, with projects ranging from luxury condo towers in Long Island City to new single-family builds across Nassau County. Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate helps buyers navigate the new construction market and negotiate directly with developers.

Where New Construction Is Happening

Queens New Developments

  • Long Island City: The epicenter of Queens new development with luxury high-rise condos, waterfront living, and modern amenities
  • Flushing: New condo developments catering to the growing Asian community, with mixed-use projects along Main Street
  • Jamaica: Major rezoning and infrastructure investment driving new residential and mixed-use construction
  • Astoria: Boutique condo developments and townhouse builds on formerly industrial lots
  • Ozone Park and South Ozone Park: New two-family and three-family homes being built for multi-generational South Asian and Guyanese families

Long Island New Construction

  • Hicksville: Transit-oriented development near the LIRR hub with new condos and townhouses
  • Hempstead: Downtown revitalization bringing new residential construction
  • Farmingdale and Massapequa: New single-family home builds in established suburban communities

Advantages of Buying New Construction

  • Customization: Choose finishes, layouts, and upgrades during the pre-construction phase
  • Modern building codes: Energy efficiency, better insulation, and modern safety features
  • Lower maintenance costs: New systems, appliances, and construction mean fewer repairs for years
  • Developer incentives: Closing cost credits, tax abatements, and sponsor financing options
  • Warranty protection: New construction typically comes with builder warranties

What to Watch Out For

New construction purchases come with unique considerations. Construction delays are common, and the final product may differ from renderings. It is critical to have experienced representation — developers have their own sales teams working in their interest, not yours. Nitin Gadura represents buyers in new construction negotiations, ensuring you get fair pricing, proper contract protections, and quality assurance throughout the process.

New Construction Buyer Representation — Expert Insights from Nitin Gadura

One of the most overlooked facts in real estate: developers have their own sales agents on-site who work exclusively in the developer's interest. Their job is to sell you that unit at the maximum possible price with the most favorable terms for the builder. As a buyer, walking into a new construction sales office without your own representation is the equivalent of walking into a lawsuit without a lawyer.

Here's what changes when Nitin Gadura represents you in a new construction purchase:

1. Price Negotiation Is Possible — Even in Competitive New Developments

Many buyers assume the price on new construction is fixed. It often isn't. In slower market conditions or during early pre-sale phases, developers frequently offer price reductions, upgrade credits, or closing cost contributions. Nitin has negotiated thousands of dollars in concessions for buyers in Long Island City, Hicksville, and Ozone Park developments. Knowing when and how to ask is the difference between a good deal and leaving money on the table.

2. Contract Review Is Critical

New construction contracts are written by developers' attorneys and strongly favor the builder. Key provisions to scrutinize include: the developer's right to delay closing indefinitely, the deposit structure (often 10-20% held for years), the scope of what "finishes" are actually included, and the transfer of warranties. An experienced agent working alongside your real estate attorney catches issues before you sign, not after.

3. Tax Abatements and Their Expiration

Many new condo developments in Queens and Long Island benefit from 421-a tax abatements, which can significantly reduce annual property taxes — sometimes by 50-80% — for up to 25 years. However, these abatements expire, and the transition to full assessed taxes can dramatically increase your carrying costs. Nitin ensures buyers fully understand the abatement schedule so there are no surprises three or five years into ownership.

4. Punch List and Closing Walk-Through

Before closing on any new construction, you are entitled to a thorough walk-through to document any defects, unfinished work, or deviations from the agreed specifications. This punch list must be resolved by the developer prior to or immediately after closing. Nitin accompanies buyers on these inspections and advocates for every item on the list — from improperly installed appliances to cosmetic defects in flooring and millwork.
