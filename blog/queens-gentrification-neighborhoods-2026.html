Queens Neighborhoods Seeing the Most Growth in 2026

By Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Published June 2026

Queens is the most dynamic real estate market in New York City right now. While Manhattan prices plateau and Brooklyn matures, Queens neighborhoods are delivering the kind of double-digit appreciation that creates real wealth for homeowners who buy at the right time. But not every Queens neighborhood is growing at the same rate, and understanding where the growth is concentrated, and why, is the difference between a smart purchase and a missed opportunity.

I am Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate, and I track Queens neighborhood data obsessively: sales volume, median prices, new permits, rental rates, commercial leasing activity, and transit development. This is not a listicle based on vibes. This is a data-driven analysis of the five Queens neighborhoods seeing the most meaningful real estate growth in 2026.

1. Jamaica, Queens: The Biggest Transformation Story in NYC

Jamaica is experiencing the most dramatic transformation of any Queens neighborhood, and the numbers prove it. The Downtown Jamaica redevelopment has attracted billions in public and private investment, and the ripple effects are being felt across residential real estate throughout the neighborhood.

Appreciation Data

  • Year-over-year price growth (2025-2026): 12-15%
  • Five-year cumulative appreciation: 40-50%
  • Median single-family home price (June 2026): $680,000
  • Median two-family home price: $780,000

What Is Driving Growth

Jamaica's growth is not accidental. It is the result of sustained infrastructure investment that is fundamentally changing the neighborhood's economic profile.

  • Transit hub: Jamaica is one of the best-connected neighborhoods in the entire city. The LIRR Jamaica Station, E/J/Z subway lines, AirTrain to JFK, and extensive bus network create a transportation advantage that no other mid-Queens neighborhood matches.
  • Commercial development: New mixed-use developments along Jamaica Avenue and Archer Avenue are adding retail, office space, and residential units. The York College expansion and new medical facilities create job growth.
  • Rezoning impact: City rezoning has enabled higher-density development near the transit hub, attracting developer interest and driving lot values upward.
  • Relative affordability: Despite strong appreciation, Jamaica remains significantly cheaper than western Queens neighborhoods like Astoria or Long Island City, attracting price-conscious buyers and investors.

For buyers, Jamaica offers the rare combination of strong current cash flow (for two-family investors) and significant appreciation potential. I recommend this neighborhood for buyers who want both rental income and long-term capital gains. See my full Jamaica market report for deeper analysis.

2. Long Island City: Queens' Premium Waterfront

Long Island City continues its evolution from industrial zone to one of NYC's most sought-after residential neighborhoods. While LIC's condo market captured headlines years ago, the growth story in 2026 is more nuanced and extends well beyond the waterfront towers.

Appreciation Data

  • Year-over-year condo price growth: 10-13%
  • Year-over-year townhouse/small multi-family growth: 8-11%
  • Median condo price (June 2026): $850,000
  • Median two-family home price: $1,200,000

What Is Driving Growth

  • One-stop Manhattan commute: The 7 train delivers riders to Grand Central in minutes. The E/M provide Midtown access. The G connects to Brooklyn. No other Queens neighborhood matches this commute.
  • Cultural infrastructure: MoMA PS1, Gantry Plaza State Park, and a growing restaurant and nightlife scene create the kind of neighborhood amenities that sustain long-term demand.
  • Office and tech expansion: Major companies have established Queens offices in LIC, creating a live-work-play ecosystem that supports housing demand.
  • New development pipeline: Continued new construction adds supply but also brings amenities, retail, and street-level activity that lift values for existing properties in surrounding blocks.

LIC is best suited for buyers who want a Manhattan-adjacent lifestyle at a Queens price point. The entry cost is higher than other neighborhoods on this list, but the appreciation trajectory and rental demand remain very strong. See my LIC buyer guide for detailed analysis.

3. Ridgewood: Brooklyn Quality at Queens Prices

Ridgewood is the quintessential spillover story. Sitting directly across the Brooklyn-Queens border from Bushwick, Ridgewood offers the same architectural charm, similar restaurant and cafe culture, and identical subway access at prices that remain 15-25% lower than equivalent Brooklyn neighborhoods.

Appreciation Data

  • Year-over-year price growth: 9-12%
  • Five-year cumulative appreciation: 35-45%
  • Median single-family home price: $850,000
  • Median two-family home price: $1,050,000

What Is Driving Growth

  • Brooklyn spillover effect: As Bushwick median home prices have crossed $1.2 million, buyers are discovering that walking two blocks east into Ridgewood saves $200,000 to $350,000 for equivalent properties.
  • Historic housing stock: Ridgewood's brick row houses, many from the early 1900s, are among the best-built residential properties in Queens. The Ridgewood Historic District provides character that new construction cannot replicate.
  • M and L subway access: Direct subway lines to Union Square, the East Village, and Williamsburg in 20-25 minutes.
  • Young professional influx: New restaurants, bars, and specialty shops along Myrtle Avenue and Fresh Pond Road signal the demographic shift that precedes sustained price growth.

4. Sunnyside: The Family-Friendly Growth Play

Sunnyside has long been one of Queens' best-kept secrets: a tree-lined, community-oriented neighborhood with strong schools, excellent transit, and a genuine small-town feel within the city. In 2026, the secret is getting out.

Appreciation Data

  • Year-over-year price growth: 8-11%
  • Median co-op price: $420,000
  • Median single-family home price: $780,000
  • Median two-family home price: $950,000

What Is Driving Growth

  • 7 train express service: 15-minute express ride to Grand Central. The 7 line is the backbone of Sunnyside's commuter appeal.
  • Sunnyside Yards potential: The long-discussed Sunnyside Yards development project, if realized, could add thousands of housing units and transform the neighborhood's density and amenity profile.
  • Affordability gap: Sunnyside remains significantly more affordable than neighboring Woodside and Long Island City, creating natural demand from price-sensitive buyers.
  • Community investment: Active civic associations, farmers markets, and local business investment create the walkability and neighborhood identity that drive demand.

5. Astoria: Mature Growth With Room to Run

Astoria's growth story is older than the others on this list, but do not mistake maturity for saturation. Astoria continues to deliver 7-10% annual appreciation because the fundamentals that drive real estate value continue to strengthen.

Appreciation Data

  • Year-over-year price growth: 7-10%
  • Five-year cumulative appreciation: 30-40%
  • Median condo price: $550,000
  • Median two-family home price: $1,100,000

What Is Driving Growth

  • Dining and entertainment destination: Astoria has emerged as one of NYC's premier dining neighborhoods, with Greek, Egyptian, Brazilian, Colombian, and contemporary American restaurants that draw citywide traffic.
  • Astoria Park and waterfront: The park, pool, and waterfront promenade provide quality-of-life amenities that are increasingly rare in NYC.
  • N/W subway line: Direct access to Midtown, Times Square, and lower Manhattan.
  • Demographic diversity: Astoria's mix of young professionals, families, and established communities creates balanced demand that insulates the market from single-demographic downturns.

For a deeper look at Astoria versus its Queens neighbors, see my Astoria vs. Sunnyside comparison.

How to Identify Early-Stage Growth Neighborhoods

Beyond the five neighborhoods above, savvy buyers look for leading indicators that predict future growth. Here is what to watch for:

  • Rising permit activity: An increase in new construction and renovation permits signals developer confidence in the area's future.
  • New commercial tenants: When specialty coffee shops, boutique fitness studios, and farm-to-table restaurants open, residential demand typically follows within 18-24 months.
  • Transit improvements: Planned subway extensions, new bus routes, or Citi Bike expansion all predict residential price growth.
  • Rental rate increases: Rising rents precede rising home prices. When rents in a neighborhood jump 8-10% year-over-year, purchase prices typically follow 6-12 months later.
  • Declining days on market: When homes sell faster, it signals demand is outpacing supply, a classic precursor to price increases.

What This Means for Buyers and Sellers

For Buyers

If you are buying in a growth neighborhood, speed matters. Properties in Jamaica, Ridgewood, and Sunnyside are receiving multiple offers within the first week of listing. Get pre-approved before you start searching, and work with an agent who knows the neighborhood intimately enough to identify value before other buyers do.

For Sellers

If you own property in any of these five neighborhoods, you are sitting on significant equity gains. The question is whether to sell now and capture the appreciation, or hold and bet on continued growth.
