Selling Your Home in Queens or Brooklyn: Step-by-Step Guide

By Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Updated April 2026

Selling a home in Queens or Brooklyn is a major financial decision that requires careful planning, smart pricing, and effective marketing. Whether you own a single-family home in Ozone Park, a multi-family in Jamaica, or a co-op in Forest Hills, this guide from Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate covers everything you need to know to sell quickly and for top dollar.

Step 1: Choose the Right Listing Agent

Your listing agent is the single biggest factor in how fast your home sells and how much you get for it. Look for an agent who:

  • Has deep knowledge of your specific neighborhood and property type
  • Can provide a detailed comparative market analysis (CMA) backed by recent sales data
  • Has a proven marketing plan that includes professional photography, online listings, and targeted outreach
  • Communicates proactively and is available seven days a week
  • Has strong negotiation skills and a track record of closing deals

Step 2: Price Your Home Strategically

Pricing is the most critical decision in the selling process. Overpricing leads to extended time on market, price reductions, and buyer skepticism. Underpricing leaves money on the table. The right price generates maximum interest and often leads to multiple offers.

Factors that affect your home's value in Queens and Brooklyn:

  • Location and neighborhood: Prices vary dramatically block by block
  • Property type: Single-family, multi-family, co-op, and condo all have different market dynamics
  • Condition and updates: Renovated kitchens and bathrooms command premium prices
  • Lot size and outdoor space: Private yards and driveways are highly valued in NYC
  • School district: Properties zoned for top-rated schools sell faster and for more
  • Transit access: Proximity to subway stations and express bus stops matters

Step 3: Prepare Your Home for Sale

First impressions drive offers. Before listing, invest in these high-return preparations:

  • Declutter and depersonalize: Remove excess furniture, personal photos, and items that make rooms feel small
  • Deep clean: Professional cleaning of floors, windows, kitchens, and bathrooms is essential
  • Minor repairs: Fix leaky faucets, squeaky doors, cracked tiles, and chipped paint
  • Fresh paint: Neutral colors make rooms feel larger and appeal to the widest range of buyers
  • Curb appeal: Clean walkways, trim landscaping, and make the entrance inviting
  • Staging: Professional staging can increase sale price by 5-10% and reduce time on market

Step 4: Market Your Property Aggressively

Effective marketing puts your home in front of the maximum number of qualified buyers. Nitin Gadura's marketing strategy includes:

  • Professional HDR photography and virtual tours
  • Listing on MLS, Zillow, Realtor.com, StreetEasy, and all major platforms
  • Targeted social media advertising to buyers searching in your neighborhood
  • Open houses and broker opens to generate buzz
  • Direct outreach to Gadura Real Estate's buyer database
  • Multilingual marketing to reach Queens' and Brooklyn's diverse buyer pool

Step 5: Review Offers and Negotiate

When offers come in, your agent should help you evaluate each one beyond just the price. Key factors to consider:

  • Buyer's financing: Cash offers and conventional loans are stronger than FHA in competitive situations
  • Pre-approval vs. pre-qualification: Pre-approved buyers are further along in the process
  • Contingencies: Fewer contingencies mean a smoother path to closing
  • Closing timeline: Does the buyer's timeline align with your needs?
  • Earnest money deposit: Higher deposits signal a more serious buyer

Step 6: Navigate the Closing Process

After accepting an offer, the closing process in Queens and Brooklyn typically takes 60-90 days. During this period:

  • The buyer will conduct a home inspection (for houses and condos)
  • The buyer's lender will order an appraisal
  • Attorneys for both sides will negotiate contract terms
  • Title search and insurance will be completed
  • For co-ops, the buyer must submit a board package and interview
  • Final walkthrough occurs 24-48 hours before closing

Seller Closing Costs in Queens and Brooklyn

Sellers in NYC face significant closing costs. Budget for:

  • Real estate commission: Typically 5-6% of the sale price, split between listing and buyer agents
  • NYC transfer tax: 1% for sales under $500,000; 1.425% for sales at or above $500,000
  • NYS transfer tax: $2 per $500 of sale price (0.4%)
  • Attorney fees: $2,000-$3,500
  • Co-op flip tax: If applicable, typically 1-3% of sale price
  • Mortgage payoff and satisfaction fees

For a detailed breakdown of what you will net from your sale, see our NYC Closing Costs Explained article.

Selling a Co-op vs. a House in Queens

Selling a co-op has unique requirements compared to selling a house or condo. Co-op sellers must factor in the board approval timeline for the buyer (add 4-8 weeks), potential flip taxes, and any subletting restrictions that may limit your buyer pool. Houses and condos generally sell faster because there is no board process.
