Best Mortgage Lenders in Queens, NY — 2026 Rates & Reviews

By Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Published June 2026

Finding the right mortgage lender can save you tens of thousands of dollars over the life of your loan. In Queens, where home prices range from $500,000 to over $1 million depending on the neighborhood and property type, even a small difference in interest rate or closing costs adds up significantly. This guide breaks down the best mortgage lenders serving Queens buyers in 2026, the loan programs available, and exactly how to compare them to get the best deal.

Types of Mortgage Lenders Available in Queens

Before comparing specific institutions, it helps to understand the categories of lenders and what each brings to the table.

National Banks

Large institutions like Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America, and Citibank have branch locations throughout Queens and offer a full range of mortgage products. Their advantages include brand stability, established processes, and sometimes competitive rates for customers with existing accounts. Their drawbacks can include slower processing, less flexibility on edge-case applications, and less personalized service.

Local and Regional Banks

Queens-based and New York-based banks like New York Community Bank (now Flagstar), Investors Bank, Flushing Bank, and Cross County Federal Savings often provide more personalized service, faster local processing, and willingness to work with borrowers who need more flexible underwriting. They know the Queens market intimately and are less likely to be confused by the quirks of NYC real estate, such as co-op purchases, HDFC buildings, or unusual property types.

Credit Unions

Queens-area credit unions, including Municipal Credit Union (MCU) and Bethpage Federal Credit Union, often offer lower rates and reduced fees compared to for-profit banks. Membership requirements vary, but many are open to anyone who lives, works, or worships in Queens or New York City. Credit unions are particularly strong for borrowers with good-but-not-excellent credit who need competitive terms.

Mortgage Brokers

Mortgage brokers do not lend money directly. Instead, they shop your application across multiple wholesale lenders to find the best rate and terms. A good broker in Queens can be invaluable because they have access to lenders who specialize in the types of properties common here: two-family homes, mixed-use buildings, co-ops, and properties in FEMA flood zones. The trade-off is that brokers charge either a fee or receive compensation from the lender, which can sometimes offset the rate savings.

Online and Fintech Lenders

Companies like Better Mortgage, Rocket Mortgage (Quicken Loans), and LoanDepot offer streamlined digital applications and sometimes aggressive initial rate quotes. Their advantage is speed and convenience for straightforward applications. Their weakness in Queens is that they often lack understanding of NYC-specific real estate complexities. Co-op purchases, in particular, can be problematic with online lenders unfamiliar with the process.

FHA vs. Conventional Loans: Which Is Better for Queens?

This is one of the most common questions I hear from first-time buyers in Queens. Here is how to decide.

Choose FHA If:

  • Your credit score is between 580 and 719
  • You have limited savings (3.5% down is the minimum)
  • You are buying a two-family home and want to count projected rental income toward qualification
  • You have a higher debt-to-income ratio (FHA allows up to 56.9% in some cases)
  • You have had past credit issues (bankruptcy, foreclosure) but have reestablished credit

Choose Conventional If:

  • Your credit score is 720 or higher
  • You can put 10-20% down (avoids or reduces PMI)
  • You want PMI to drop off automatically at 20% equity (FHA MIP is for the life of the loan)
  • The property is a co-op (most co-ops do not accept FHA financing)
  • You are buying a higher-priced property above FHA loan limits

2026 FHA Loan Limits for Queens

FHA loan limits for Queens County (which is part of the New York-Newark-Jersey City MSA) for 2026:

  • 1-unit: $1,149,825
  • 2-unit: $1,472,250
  • 3-unit: $1,779,525
  • 4-unit: $2,211,600

These high limits mean FHA is a viable option for most Queens purchases, including two-family homes.

SONYMA Programs for Queens First-Time Buyers

The State of New York Mortgage Agency (SONYMA) offers some of the best mortgage programs available to Queens buyers. If you qualify, SONYMA should be at the top of your list. Read our detailed SONYMA guide here.

SONYMA Low Interest Rate Program

  • Below-market fixed interest rates (typically 0.5-1% below conventional rates)
  • 30-year fixed-rate terms
  • Available for 1-4 unit properties in Queens
  • Income limits apply (varies by household size and county)

SONYMA Down Payment Assistance Loan (DPAL)

  • Up to $15,000 toward down payment and closing costs
  • 0% interest, no monthly payments
  • Repaid when you sell, refinance, or pay off your first mortgage
  • Stacks with the SONYMA low interest rate first mortgage

SONYMA Eligibility for Queens

  • Must be a first-time buyer (no homeownership in the past 3 years)
  • Must meet income limits for Queens County
  • Must complete a homebuyer education course
  • Property must meet SONYMA purchase price limits
  • Must use a SONYMA-participating lender

How to Compare Mortgage Lenders: Beyond the Interest Rate

Most buyers focus exclusively on the interest rate. That is a mistake. Here is what to compare across all lenders:

Total Closing Costs

Request a Loan Estimate (LE) from each lender. The LE is a standardized federal form that shows all costs, making true comparisons possible. Pay attention to:

  • Origination fees: Some lenders charge 0.5-1% of the loan amount; others charge nothing
  • Discount points: Prepaid interest that buys a lower rate. Make sure you are comparing rates with the same point structure
  • Lender credits: Some lenders offer credits that offset closing costs in exchange for a slightly higher rate
  • Third-party fees: Appraisal, title insurance, attorney fees. These should be similar across lenders, but check for inflated estimates

Processing Speed and Reliability

In Queens' competitive market, a lender who cannot close on time can cost you the deal. Ask each lender:

  • What is your average time from application to closing?
  • What percentage of your loans close on the originally scheduled date?
  • How many loans does each processor handle simultaneously?

Rate Lock Terms

Most lenders offer 30, 45, or 60-day rate locks. Understand:

  • How long is the lock period?
  • What does a lock extension cost if closing is delayed?
  • Can you float down if rates drop after locking?

Responsiveness

Ask your real estate agent which lenders they prefer working with. Agents see lender performance from the other side of the transaction and know which lenders consistently deliver and which ones cause problems. This real-world insight is more valuable than any online review.

Tips for Getting Approved for a Mortgage in Queens

1. Check Your Credit Early

Pull your credit reports from all three bureaus at least 3-6 months before applying. Dispute any errors and pay down revolving balances to below 30% of credit limits. Every point of credit score improvement can save you money over the life of the loan.

2. Gather Documentation

Lenders will need:

  • Two years of W-2s or tax returns
  • Recent pay stubs (30 days)
  • Two months of bank statements (all pages)
  • Government-issued ID
  • Explanation letters for any large deposits, gaps in employment, or credit events

3. Avoid Major Financial Changes

Once you start the mortgage process, do not:

  • Open new credit cards or take on new debt
  • Make large purchases (car, furniture, appliances)
  • Change jobs if possible
  • Make large undocumented cash deposits
  • Co-sign anyone else's loan

4. Get Pre-Approved, Not Just Pre-Qualified

A pre-qualification is an estimate. A pre-approval involves actual verification of your income, assets, and credit. In Queens' competitive market, sellers and their agents strongly prefer offers from pre-approved buyers. A pre-approval letter from a reputable local lender carries more weight than one from an unknown online lender.

5. Special Considerations for Self-Employed Buyers

Queens has a high concentration of self-employed individuals, small business owners, and 1099 contractors. If you are self-employed, expect lenders to require:

  • Two full years of personal and business tax returns
  • Year-to-date profit and loss statement
  • Business bank statements
  • Business license or registration

Local Queens lenders and credit unions are often more flexible with self-employed borrowers than large national banks. A mortgage broker can also help find lenders that specialize in self-employed borrower programs, including bank statement loans that use 12-24 months of deposits rather than tax returns.

Special Programs for Queens Homebuyers

Beyond standard FHA and conventional loans, Queens buyers may qualify for:

  • HomeFirst Down Payment Assistance Program (HPD): Up to $100,000 in down payment assistance for qualifying NYC buyers. Forgiven after 10-15 years of owner occupancy
  • SONYMA programs: Below-market rates and up to $15,000 in down payment assistance
  • VA loans: 0% down for qualifying veterans. Multiple Queens lenders are experienced with VA loans
  • USDA loans: Not available in Queens (urban area), but available in parts of eastern Long Island
  • Good Neighbor Next Door (HUD): 50% discount on selected HUD homes for teachers, law enforcement, firefighters, and EMTs
  • Employer assistance programs: Several NYC-area employers offer homeownership assistance benefits. Check with your HR department
