Why You Should Have a Real Estate Broker

A licensed New York State real estate broker is the professional whose fiduciary duty is to represent your interests — buyer or seller — through pricing, marketing, negotiation, and transaction coordination. Pick your area below for a neighborhood-specific guide.

We strongly encourage every buyer and seller to also retain their own independent, New York–licensed real estate attorney for the legal portion of the transaction. Your broker handles the market; your attorney handles contract, title, and closing. A good broker and a good attorney work together.

What a Broker Does That Online Portals Cannot

  • Negotiation on price, contingencies, and repair credits — where most of the real money is made or lost
  • Accurate pricing based on private sold comps and block-level data, not estimates
  • Access to off-market and pre-MLS inventory
  • Fiduciary duty under New York State license law (loyalty, disclosure, confidentiality, accounting, reasonable care)
  • Coordination with your attorney, lender, title company, and inspector
