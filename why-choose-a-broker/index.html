What a Broker Does That a Portal Cannot

  • Negotiation on price, contingencies, and repair credits — where most of the real money is made or lost
  • Accurate pricing based on documented sold comps and block-level data, not estimates
  • Access to off-market and pre-MLS inventory
  • Fiduciary duty under New York State Real Property Law Article 12-A: loyalty, disclosure, confidentiality, accounting, reasonable care [1]
  • Coordination with your attorney, lender, title company, and inspector
  • Fair Housing Act and NY State Human Rights Law compliance on every transaction [2][3]
Always retain your own New York–licensed real estate attorney for the legal portion of your transaction. Your broker handles the market; your attorney handles the contract, title, and closing. A good broker and a good attorney work together — neither replaces the other.
