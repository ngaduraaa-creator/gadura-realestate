Howard Beach Rental Market Overview

Howard Beach offers something rare in New York City: a waterfront neighborhood with a suburban pace of life and a direct subway connection to Manhattan. Nestled along Jamaica Bay between JFK Airport and the Belt Parkway, Howard Beach has historically been an owner-occupied community of single-family and two-family homes. That means the rental market is tighter here than in denser Queens neighborhoods, and many of the best apartments are filled through broker relationships before they ever hit public listing sites. Why Rent in Howard Beach?

  • Waterfront lifestyle: Jamaica Bay waterfront parks, marinas, and the Gateway National Recreation Area offer kayaking, fishing, birdwatching, and sunset views that feel miles away from the city
  • Suburban quiet in NYC: Tree-lined streets with well-maintained detached homes, low crime rates, and a tight-knit community atmosphere make Howard Beach one of the most livable neighborhoods in southern Queens
  • JFK Airport proximity: The AirTrain connects directly from the Howard Beach A train station to all JFK terminals, making this neighborhood ideal for airline employees, airport workers, and frequent travelers
  • Strong dining scene: Cross Bay Boulevard features established Italian restaurants, pizzerias, delis, and bakeries alongside newer additions serving diverse cuisines
  • A train express access: The Howard Beach–JFK Airport station on the A line provides a direct ride to Lower Manhattan and connections to Midtown

Types of Rentals Available in Howard Beach

Howard Beach has a smaller but high-quality rental inventory compared to denser Queens neighborhoods:

  • Two-family house units: The primary rental type in Howard Beach — full-floor apartments in owner-occupied two-family homes, often featuring private driveways, separate entrances, and laundry hookups
  • Basement apartments: Legal lower-level units with separate entrances in the neighborhood's extensive two-family stock, typically the most affordable option
  • Single-family house rentals: Occasionally available detached homes with yards and driveways, ranging from $2,800 to $3,500 per month for three- and four-bedroom configurations
  • New construction units: A limited number of newer two-family and three-family builds along Cross Bay Boulevard offer modern finishes and updated layouts

Transit & Commute from Howard Beach

Howard Beach is anchored by a single but strategically important transit station:

  • A train: Howard Beach–JFK Airport station provides direct service to Lower Manhattan (Fulton Street), Midtown (42nd Street–Port Authority via transfer), and connections throughout Brooklyn
  • AirTrain JFK: Free transfer from the A train platform to all JFK Airport terminals — a major advantage for airport employees and travelers
  • Bus routes: Q11 runs through Howard Beach connecting to Woodhaven Boulevard and northern neighborhoods; Q21 provides east-west service along Cross Bay Boulevard
  • Belt Parkway: Direct highway access for drivers commuting to Brooklyn, Staten Island (via the Cross Bay Bridge and Marine Parkway Bridge), or Long Island
  • Cross Bay Boulevard: The main commercial and commuter corridor connecting Howard Beach to Broad Channel and the Rockaways
