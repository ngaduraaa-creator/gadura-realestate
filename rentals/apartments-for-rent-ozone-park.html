Ozone Park Rental Market Overview

Ozone Park remains one of South Queens' most sought-after neighborhoods for renters seeking affordable apartments with strong community ties. With a thriving Indo-Caribbean and South Asian population, tree-lined residential blocks, and direct A train service to Manhattan, Ozone Park delivers genuine neighborhood living at rents well below the borough-wide average. Why Rent in Ozone Park?

  • Affordable rents: Ozone Park consistently offers lower rents than Astoria, Long Island City, and Forest Hills while delivering solid transit access and neighborhood amenities
  • Vibrant cultural community: Liberty Avenue is the heart of the Indo-Caribbean corridor with Guyanese, Trinidadian, Indian, and Bangladeshi restaurants, grocery stores, and businesses
  • Direct subway service: The A train runs through Ozone Park with multiple stations providing a roughly 45-minute commute to Midtown Manhattan
  • Proximity to JFK Airport: Ideal for frequent travelers or airport workers, JFK is minutes away via the AirTrain from Howard Beach station
  • Family-friendly blocks: Quiet residential streets with local parks including Joseph P. Addabbo Memorial Park and Centreville Playground

Types of Rentals Available in Ozone Park

The Ozone Park rental market features several property types to match different budgets and living preferences:

  • Walk-up apartments: Three- and four-story buildings along Rockaway Boulevard and major cross streets, typically offering studios through two-bedroom units
  • Two-family house units: The most common rental type in Ozone Park — first-floor or second-floor apartments in owner-occupied two-family homes with private entrances
  • Basement apartments: Legal basement units with separate entrances, common in the two-family housing stock throughout Ozone Park and South Ozone Park
  • Single-family house rentals: Occasionally available for larger families, three- and four-bedroom detached houses rent in the $2,800–$3,500 range

Transit & Commute from Ozone Park

Ozone Park offers solid public transit options for commuters throughout New York City:

  • A train: Ozone Park–Lefferts Blvd (terminal), 80th Street, 88th Street, and Rockaway Boulevard stations serve the neighborhood
  • J/Z trains: Accessible from Woodhaven Boulevard station, connecting to downtown Brooklyn and Lower Manhattan
  • AirTrain JFK: Board at Howard Beach station (one stop on the A) for direct JFK Airport access
  • Bus routes: Q11, Q21, Q37, Q41, and Q53 SBS connect Ozone Park to Woodhaven, Richmond Hill, Howard Beach, and Rego Park
  • Major roads: Cross Bay Boulevard, Woodhaven Boulevard, and the Belt Parkway provide car-commute flexibility
