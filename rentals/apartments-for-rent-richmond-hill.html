Richmond Hill Rental Market Overview

Richmond Hill stands as one of Queens' most culturally rich neighborhoods, anchored by a large Guyanese, Indo-Caribbean, Punjabi Sikh, and South Asian population that gives Liberty Avenue and Lefferts Boulevard their distinctive character. Renters flock to Richmond Hill for its combination of affordable pricing, strong community bonds, and proximity to Jamaica's transit hub. Why Rent in Richmond Hill?

  • Budget-friendly rents: Richmond Hill consistently ranks among the most affordable rental neighborhoods in Queens, offering spacious apartments at prices significantly below the borough average
  • Cultural epicenter: Liberty Avenue between Lefferts Blvd and 133rd Street is the heart of the Indo-Caribbean community, with roti shops, halal grocers, sari boutiques, and Guyanese bakeries within walking distance
  • Gurdwara and temple access: Home to several prominent Sikh gurdwaras including the Richmond Hill Gurdwara on 118th Street, plus Hindu mandirs and Islamic centers throughout the neighborhood
  • Convenient transit: The J/Z train provides a direct shot to downtown Brooklyn and Lower Manhattan, while buses connect to Jamaica Center and the E/F subway lines
  • Strong rental stock: Predominantly two-family and three-family homes built in the early-to-mid 20th century, offering full-floor apartments with generous square footage

Types of Rentals Available in Richmond Hill

Richmond Hill's housing stock provides a variety of rental options for individuals and families:

  • Two-family house apartments: The dominant rental type — full-floor units in owner-occupied homes with separate entrances, often including yard access or driveway parking
  • Three-family house units: Multi-floor walk-ups common along the major avenues, typically offering one- and two-bedroom configurations
  • Basement apartments: Legal basement units with egress windows and separate entrances, popular with single renters and couples for their lower price point
  • Whole-house rentals: Three- and four-bedroom detached homes occasionally available in the $2,600–$3,200 range for larger families
  • Rooms for rent: Shared housing arrangements common among students and newcomers, typically $800–$1,200 per month including utilities

Transit & Commute from Richmond Hill

Richmond Hill provides several transit options connecting residents to the rest of New York City:

  • J/Z trains: Stations at 111th Street, 104th-102nd Streets, and Lefferts Boulevard provide service to downtown Brooklyn (Fulton Street) and Lower Manhattan (Broad Street)
  • Bus connections: Q10 to JFK Airport and Kew Gardens, Q37 to Ridgewood, Q41 along Lefferts Blvd, Q56 to Jamaica Center, Q60 to Long Island City
  • Jamaica transit hub: A short bus ride connects to Jamaica Center with access to the E, J, Z subway lines, LIRR, and AirTrain JFK
  • Major roadways: Atlantic Avenue, Jamaica Avenue, and Lefferts Boulevard provide direct car access to Brooklyn, Jamaica, and the Van Wyck Expressway
