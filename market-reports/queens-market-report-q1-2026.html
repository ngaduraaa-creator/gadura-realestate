Q1 2026 Overview

Queens continues to demonstrate broad-based appreciation across its diverse neighborhoods in Q1 2026. Every ZIP code surveyed in this report showed positive year-over-year price growth, ranging from +5.3% in Jamaica to +3.1% in select high-demand corridors. The median home value across these 10 ZIP codes averaged $705,500, with typical rents running $2,580/mo.

The borough benefits from a structural supply constraint — limited new construction relative to population density — combined with its role as the most ethnically diverse county in the United States. Demand from first-generation homebuyers, multi-generational families, and investors continues to support pricing across all property types.

Key Takeaways for Q1 2026

  • All 10 ZIP codes positive YoY — Queens remains a broad-based appreciating market with no pockets of decline in this survey.
  • Highest growth in Jamaica and Woodhaven — these value-oriented neighborhoods are attracting first-time buyers priced out of more expensive ZIP codes.
  • Premium neighborhoods hold strong — Douglaston ($820,000) and Long Island City continue to command top-tier pricing.
  • Rental market tight — average rent across surveyed ZIPs is $2,580/mo, supporting strong cap rates for multi-family investors.
  • Rate sensitivity remains high — every 1% move in the 30-year fixed rate shifts affordability meaningfully across all price tiers.
