Frequently Asked Questions About Buying a Home on Long Island, NY

Expert answers from Nitin Gadura | Gadura Real Estate | Updated April 2026

Long Island offers some of the finest suburban living in the New York metropolitan area, with excellent schools, beautiful beaches, and a wide range of communities from vibrant downtown villages to quiet cul-de-sac neighborhoods. Whether you are moving from the city, relocating from out of state, or upgrading within the Island, understanding the Long Island market is essential. Below, Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate answers the most common questions about buying a home on Long Island.

How much does it cost to buy a home on Long Island?

The median home price on Long Island is approximately $695,000 as of early 2026. Nassau County tends to be more expensive with a median around $740,000, while Suffolk County is somewhat more affordable with a median around $600,000. Prices range widely — from under $400,000 in parts of central Suffolk to over $2 million in prestigious North Shore Gold Coast communities like Old Westbury and Oyster Bay. Nitin Gadura of Gadura Real Estate provides free market analyses for any Long Island community so you can budget accurately.

Can I buy a house on Long Island with no money down?

Yes, it is possible with the right loan program. VA loans offer zero down payment for eligible veterans and active-duty military. USDA loans may apply in qualifying rural areas of eastern Suffolk County. Additionally, SONYMA and other New York State programs offer down payment assistance for first-time buyers who meet income requirements. Most conventional purchases require 5-20% down, with 10-20% being most common on Long Island. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate can connect you with lenders who specialize in low- and no-down-payment programs for Long Island buyers.

What are property taxes like on Long Island?

Long Island has some of the highest property taxes in the nation, which is the most important budget factor many buyers overlook. Annual property taxes typically range from $8,000 to $20,000+ depending on the community, school district, and assessed home value. Nassau County averages around $13,000-$15,000 per year, while Suffolk County averages $10,000-$12,000. The STAR exemption can reduce your school tax burden by $800-$1,400 annually. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate always provides property tax data for every listing so buyers can calculate their true monthly housing costs.

What are the best school districts on Long Island?

Long Island is home to some of the best public school districts in New York State, which is a major driver of home values. Top-rated districts include Jericho, Syosset, Great Neck, Manhasset, and Roslyn in Nassau County, and Cold Spring Harbor, Half Hollow Hills, and Northport-East Northport in Suffolk County. School district quality significantly affects home values — homes in top-rated districts command a 15-25% premium over similar homes in average districts. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate helps families identify communities with the best schools at every price point.

Is it better to buy in Nassau County or Suffolk County?

Nassau County offers shorter commutes to NYC (30-50 minutes via LIRR), more suburban density, walkable downtown villages, and proximity to Queens. However, it comes with higher home prices and property taxes. Suffolk County offers more land, larger homes, beautiful beaches, wine country, and lower prices, but commutes to Manhattan can be 60-90+ minutes. The choice depends on your commute needs, budget, and lifestyle preferences. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate serves both counties and can help you weigh the tradeoffs based on your specific priorities.

How long is the commute from Long Island to Manhattan?

LIRR commute times from western Nassau County stations (Great Neck, Manhasset, Floral Park) to Penn Station are 30-45 minutes. Central Nassau towns like Garden City and Mineola are about 40-50 minutes. Western Suffolk towns like Huntington and Babylon are 55-70 minutes. Eastern Suffolk destinations like Patchogue and Riverhead can be 90+ minutes. Monthly LIRR passes cost $250-$400 depending on zone. Many Long Island employers are located on the Island itself, eliminating the NYC commute entirely. Nitin Gadura factors commute time and cost into every Long Island home recommendation he makes.

What are closing costs when buying a house on Long Island?

Buyer closing costs on Long Island typically range from 2-5% of the purchase price. Major costs include the mortgage recording tax (approximately 1% in Nassau and Suffolk counties), attorney fees ($2,500-$4,000), title insurance, land survey ($500-$800), home inspection, and lender fees. The NYS mansion tax of 1% applies to purchases over $1 million. Unlike NYC, there is no city transfer tax on Long Island. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate provides detailed closing cost estimates for every Long Island property his clients consider so there are no surprises.

Do I need a home inspection on Long Island?

A home inspection is not legally required in New York but is absolutely essential when buying on Long Island. Many Long Island homes were built in the 1950s-1970s and may have issues with foundations, aging heating systems, roofing, plumbing, electrical systems, or underground oil tanks that can cost thousands to remediate. A thorough inspection typically costs $500-$800 and can save you tens of thousands in unexpected repairs. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate recommends trusted local inspectors and helps negotiate repair credits based on inspection findings.

What about flood zones on Long Island?

Flood zones are a significant consideration on Long Island, particularly along the South Shore, barrier islands (Long Beach, Fire Island), and waterfront communities in both Nassau and Suffolk counties. Properties in FEMA flood zones require flood insurance, which can cost $1,500-$10,000+ annually depending on elevation and zone designation. Post-Sandy building codes have improved resilience, but flood risk remains a major factor in pricing, insurance costs, and long-term value. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate checks flood zone status for every property and helps buyers understand the full financial implications.

Is Long Island a good investment for real estate?

Long Island has historically been a strong real estate market with steady appreciation driven by limited buildable land, excellent school districts, and proximity to NYC. While high property taxes cut into rental yields, appreciation has averaged 3-5% annually over the past decade. Multi-family properties and homes with legal accessory apartments can provide rental income to offset carrying costs. Areas near planned transit improvements, like the proposed Third Track LIRR expansion, may see above-average appreciation. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate helps investors analyze Long Island opportunities for the strongest return potential.

What is a mother-daughter home on Long Island?

A mother-daughter home (also called an accessory apartment or in-law suite) is a single-family home with a separate, self-contained living space — typically with its own entrance, kitchen, bathroom, and sometimes its own utilities. These are extremely popular on Long Island for multi-generational living or supplemental rental income. However, legality depends on local municipal zoning — not all towns allow them, and some require permits. Nitin Gadura at Gadura Real Estate can help you find legal mother-daughter properties and navigate the zoning requirements in your target Long Island community.

How do I choose the right Long Island community?

Choosing the right Long Island community depends on your commute tolerance, school district preferences, budget, and lifestyle. Walkable downtowns like Garden City, Rockville Centre, and Port Washington appeal to those who want a village feel with shops and restaurants. Quieter, more spacious suburbs like Massapequa, Levittown, and Commack offer more home for the money. Beach communities like Long Beach appeal to waterfront lovers. North Shore communities offer wooded, hilly terrain and historic charm.
