The site uses a "hub and spoke" architecture where borough-level pillar pages (Queens, Brooklyn, Long Island) link to every neighborhood page within that borough. This creates:
- **Topical authority** for each borough
- **Internal link equity** flowing from hub to individual pages
- **Crawl efficiency** for search engine bots
- **User navigation** paths that keep visitors engaged

## INTERNAL LINKING STRATEGY

Every page participates in the internal linking web:
1. **Homepage** links to borough hubs, community pages, featured blogs, services
2. **Borough hubs** link to every neighborhood within that borough
3. **Neighborhood pages** link back to borough hub + related community pages
4. **Community pages** link to relevant neighborhoods where that community lives
5. **Blog articles** link to neighborhood pages, community pages, and service pages
6. **Service pages** link to relevant neighborhoods and blog content
7. **FAQ pages** link to related blog articles and service pages
8. **Footer** on every page links to top neighborhoods, communities, and resources

This creates a **dense internal link graph** that passes authority across the entire site and helps search engines understand the topical relationships between pages.
