> 🧵 If you're trying to buy your first home in Queens NY in 2026, here's the checklist nobody gives you:
>
> 1/ Pull all 3 credit scores at AnnualCreditReport.com BEFORE you talk to any lender.
> 2/ Get pre-approved with 2–3 lenders. The rate spread is real.
> 3/ FHA goes down to 580 / 3.5% down. SONYMA stacks on top with up to $15K DPA grant.
> 4/ Co-op vs condo vs single-family — totally different deal mechanics. Pick before touring.
> 5/ Sign a buyer-broker agreement (required since Aug 2024) but negotiate the commission.
> 6/ Tour 5–10 places before any offer to calibrate.
> 7/ Get inspector + attorney lined up before going under contract — NY uses lawyers, not title companies.

> Got asked today: "Can I buy a Queens 2-family with FHA at 5% down?"
> Yes — FHA self-sufficiency rule means the rental income from the OTHER unit has to cover ~75% of the mortgage. For 2-fam Ozone Park ($800K range) you usually need rents at $2,200+ on the other side. Doable.
