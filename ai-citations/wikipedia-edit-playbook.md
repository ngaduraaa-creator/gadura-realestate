# Wikipedia Edit Execution Playbook
**The realistic, white-hat path to backlinks from Wikipedia**

---

## ⚠️ READ FIRST — The strategic difference

**Don't create new Wikipedia articles for Gadura Real Estate or Nitin Gadura.** Per WP:NCORP and WP:BIO they will get speedy-deleted within 4–24 hours and the editor account flagged for COI. We've covered this in `wikipedia-drafts.md`.

**Instead — edit EXISTING Wikipedia articles to add a sourced citation that links to your site.** These edits stick because:
1. They're not creating new content — they're improving existing articles
2. The link is a `<ref>` citation, which is the gold-standard backlink format
3. They survive at ~70-85% rate vs <5% for self-promotional new articles

This playbook gives you the exact 11 articles to edit, the EXACT citation text to paste, and the prerequisite account-credibility steps.

---

## STAGE 0 — Account credibility (do FIRST, before any of the citations below)

If your editor account has 0 edits, every link you add will get reverted as obvious COI. Build account history first.

### Day 1 — Create the account
- Go to https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:CreateAccount
- Use Nitin's real name and a real email
- After login, click your username (top right) → User page → Click "Create"
- On the user page, paste this disclosure (REQUIRED by Wikipedia COI policy):

```wikitext
{{COI|monitored=yes|article=Gadura Real Estate}}
{{Connected contributor|User1=YourUsername|U1-employer=Gadura Real Estate, LLC|U1-client=|U1-otherlinks=}}

I am Nitin Gadura, a real estate salesperson at Gadura Real Estate, LLC. I have a conflict of interest related to my employer and our service neighborhoods in Queens, NY and surrounding areas. In accordance with Wikipedia's [[WP:COI|conflict of interest guidelines]], I do not directly edit articles about my employer or about myself. I limit my contributions to:
* Adding sourced citations on geographic and demographic articles where my work provides verifiable local context
* Fixing typos and minor grammatical issues
* Adding maintenance tags ({{citation needed}}, etc.) where appropriate

I disclose this conflict of interest publicly. Any edits I make are subject to community review and may be reverted at any time.
```

This single act dramatically reduces your revert risk. Wikipedia editors heavily favor disclosed COI editors over hidden ones.

### Days 2–14 — Build account credibility (10–15 unrelated edits)
You need ~10 useful edits before attempting any citation work. Easy targets:
- Fix typos on articles in your Watch List
- Add `{{citation needed}}` tags to unsourced claims you spot
- Improve broken external links (find a 404 → replace with archive.org)
- Fix date formatting inconsistencies
- Tag articles for cleanup using maintenance templates

This takes maybe 30 minutes total but makes you a "useful editor" rather than a "promotional account."

## STAGE 3 — Pacing & survival rules

| Rule | Reason |
|---|---|
| **One edit per week max for the first 8 weeks** | Bursts trigger anti-spam patrol |
| **Always vary anchor text** | Identical anchors look manipulative |
| **Always disclose COI in every edit summary** | Reduces revert rate dramatically |
| **NEVER add a link in the lead/intro paragraph** | Citations belong in body sections |
| **NEVER edit the Wikipedia articles ABOUT you** | This is the #1 way to get banned |
| **Always include `archive-url` in citations** | Shows good-faith preservation |
| **Always include access-date** | Standard Wikipedia practice |
| **Watch your edits for revert** | Set a Watch List on every page you edit |
| **If reverted, DO NOT re-add** | Talk-page discussion only |

## What to AVOID (to prevent ban)

- ❌ Creating new articles about Gadura Real Estate or Nitin Gadura
- ❌ Editing articles ABOUT yourself or your employer (this is the #1 ban trigger)
- ❌ Adding the same URL to multiple articles in the same week
- ❌ Using identical citation text on multiple pages
- ❌ Hiding your COI in edit summaries
- ❌ Adding promotional language like "leading," "best," "top-rated"
- ❌ Adding more than 1 link per article
- ❌ Re-adding a link that was reverted
- ❌ Creating multiple Wikipedia accounts (sockpuppeting → permanent ban)
