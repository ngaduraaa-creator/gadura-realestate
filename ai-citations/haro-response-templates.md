## Response Rules

1. **Respond within 4 hours of the query.** Reporters use the first 3–5 responses they get. Speed beats brilliance.
2. **First sentence = the quotable line.** Reporters scan; if your opener works as a pull-quote, you're in.
3. **Stay under 200 words.** Long replies get skipped.
4. **Always include credentials in the opener** — "As a NYS-licensed real estate broker who's closed $100M+..."
5. **Never link to your homepage in the body.** Put links in the signature block. Body links look like spam.
6. **Verifiable specifics > vague advice.** "Mansion tax kicks in at $1M" beats "there are extra NY taxes."

## TEMPLATE 1 — First-time homebuyer advice (NYC / Long Island)

> As a Licensed NYS Real Estate Salesperson who's helped 500+ first-time NY buyers close, the single biggest mistake I see is shopping with one lender. The rate spread between two FHA-experienced lenders for the *same* buyer is routinely 0.25%, which is roughly $40/month or $14,000 over a 30-year loan.
>
> Three concrete tips:
>
> 1. Pull all 3 credit scores at AnnualCreditReport.com before talking to anyone — lenders use the middle score, and 580+ unlocks FHA at 3.5% down.
> 2. SONYMA's $15K Down-Payment Assistance Loan stacks on top of FHA for first-time New York buyers — most agents either don't know about it or don't push it.
> 3. House-hacking a 2-family with FHA at 3.5% down covers most of your mortgage with the rental income from the other unit. The math works in Queens at $750–$900K.

## TEMPLATE 2 — Best time to buy/sell in 2026 (Queens, NY metro)

> The "best time to buy" question gets oversimplified to seasonal heuristics. The real answer for Queens / Long Island in 2026: buy when your DTI works, your job is stable for 2+ years, and you've been pre-approved by 2+ lenders. Spring listings sell ~3–5% over asking; winter listings sit longer and have negotiation room. Both work — for different buyer types.

## TEMPLATE 4 — Co-op vs condo (NYC specifically)

> NYC co-ops are typically 20–40% cheaper per square foot than condos in the same building age — but the financing math gets harder, the board approval can take 4–8 weeks, and resale takes longer. The right structure depends on your liquidity, timeline, and how much board scrutiny you can stomach. For a buyer with stable W-2 income, $100K+ liquid, and 2+ years on the same job, co-ops are usually the better economic choice in Forest Hills and Rego Park.

## TEMPLATE 7 — FHA loans in NYC (when they work, when they don't)

> FHA loans accept 580+ credit at 3.5% down — incredible math for first-time NY buyers — but most NYC condos and *almost no* older co-ops are FHA-approved. You have to pre-qualify the building, not just the buyer. Single-family in Queens, 2-fam house-hacking, and new-construction condos work great on FHA. Older Manhattan buildings rarely do.

## TEMPLATE 8 — 1031 exchange basics

> 1031 exchanges are one of the most powerful wealth-building tools in U.S. real estate, and they're radically underused by individual NY investors. Sell a Queens 2-family, identify replacement property within 45 days, close within 180 days — defer 100% of capital gains. We do these for Queens-to-Long-Island upgrades and for portfolio consolidation regularly. Heirs receive stepped-up basis, which means the deferred gain disappears entirely on death — that's the "forever 1031" play.

## TEMPLATE 9 — Down payment / saving strategies

> The "20% down" myth keeps NY first-time buyers renting longer than they need to. FHA: 3.5%. SONYMA: free $15K grant. HomeReady: 3%. VA: 0%. On a $725K Queens median, you can be in with $25K down + ~$30K in closing — half the "20% down" math. The cost of waiting an extra year to save 20% in a market appreciating 3%+ is bigger than the lifetime PMI/MIP cost.
