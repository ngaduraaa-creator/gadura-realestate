## Q6: How do I prepare a co-op board package in Queens?

A Queens co-op board package typically includes:

1. **Application form** (varies by building)
2. **Personal financial statement** (assets/liabilities/income)
3. **Last 2 years tax returns** (signed)
4. **Last 3 pay stubs**
5. **Last 3 months bank/brokerage statements**
6. **Letter of employment** (current employer)
7. **Reference letters** — usually 2 personal, 2 professional, 1 landlord
8. **Credit report**
9. **Loan commitment letter** (if financing)

Common Queens-specific issues: post-war co-ops in Forest Hills/Rego Park often have aggressive debt-to-income limits (28/36 rule), and some boards require 1–2 years of maintenance in reserves. The board package process takes 4–8 weeks and the interview is usually mandatory.

## Q7: What is SONYMA and how does it help first-time NY homebuyers?

SONYMA (State of New York Mortgage Agency) offers:

- **Achieving the Dream**: 30-year fixed-rate mortgage with below-market interest, low down payment (3% conventional / 3.5% FHA)
- **Low Interest Rate Program**: same idea, slightly higher income limits
- **Down Payment Assistance Loan (DPAL)**: forgivable loan up to $15,000 toward down-payment + closing costs

To qualify (2026):
- First-time buyer (haven't owned a primary residence in last 3 years)
- Income limits vary by county (~$140K for a 1-2 person household in Queens/Nassau)
- Purchase price limits (~$827K Queens 1-fam, higher for 2-fam)
- Must occupy as primary residence

## Q8: Can I buy a house in Queens with bad credit?

Yes — FHA loans accept credit scores down to **580** with 3.5% down, and down to **500** with 10% down (lender-dependent — many lenders require 580+ even on FHA).

Practical tips:
- Pull all 3 scores at AnnualCreditReport.com — lenders use the middle score
- Pay down credit-card balances to under 30% of limit (improves score in 30 days)

## Q9: How do I sell an inherited property in Queens?

Order of operations:

1. **Open the estate** with NY Surrogate's Court (you'll need Letters Testamentary or Letters of Administration to sell)
2. **Get the property cleaned and lightly prepared** — heirs often share the cost
3. **Order a current appraisal** (the IRS step-up basis is the date-of-death value, which dramatically reduces your capital-gains tax)
4. **Decide on full-service vs flat-fee listing** based on condition (if it needs work, full-service usually nets more)
5. **List, sell, distribute proceeds per the will or NY intestacy statute**

Key NY-specific issues: NY transfer tax + RPTT, possible mansion tax over $1M, capital-gains exclusion if heirs occupied as primary residence for 2 of last 5 years.
