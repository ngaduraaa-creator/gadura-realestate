What Bellerose Is Like

Bellerose sits astride the Queens-Nassau border — offering 50% single-family ownership rates, NYC property tax rates, and LIRR access at prices 20% below New Hyde Park.

Queens County Farm Museum — operating continuously since 1697 — sits on the Bellerose border and is the largest remaining tract of undisturbed farmland in New York City, covering 47 acres open to the public.

Getting Around Bellerose

Transit options: LIRR Bellerose Station (Main Line/Hempstead Branch), Q1 (Bellerose–Jamaica), Q36. Average commute to Manhattan: 38 minutes.

Practical Moving Checklist for Bellerose

  1. Update your address: USPS change of address, DMV (NY requires update within 10 days of moving), IRS, employer, bank, voter registration
  2. Utilities setup: ConEd for electricity, National Grid for gas; call 2 weeks before move-in
  3. Sanitation: NYC Sanitation handles trash/recycling — check your pickup days at NYC DSNY
  4. Schools: Register at your local district office with proof of address; school year starts early September
  5. Library card: Queens Public Library has branches throughout Bellerose — free access to books, internet, and community programs
  6. Community boards: Queens has 14 community boards; look up yours to stay informed on local issues
