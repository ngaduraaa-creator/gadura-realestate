Buying a Home in Kew Gardens, Queens NY — Complete 2026 Guide

Updated 2026-04-20 by Gadura Real Estate | Kew Gardens Buyer Specialists

Buying a home in Kew Gardens is one of the best long-term investments you can make in Queens — but only if you approach it with the right strategy. This guide walks you through every step of the process, from pre-approval to keys in hand.

Step 1: Know Your Budget

The median home price in Kew Gardens is $325,000. At today's rates (approximately 6.8–7.2% for a 30-year fixed), a 20% down payment on a $325,000 home means a down payment of $65,000 and a monthly principal + interest payment of roughly $1,820 before taxes and insurance.

Factor in annual property taxes of approximately $6,800 (0.88% effective rate in Kew Gardens, per NYC Department of Finance FY2024–2025).

Step 2: Get Pre-Approved (Before You Search)

In Kew Gardens's market — where homes average just 35 days on market — sellers expect pre-approval letters. Buyers without one are routinely passed over. Work with a lender who can close in 30–45 days. Credit unions and community banks often offer slightly better rates for Queens buyers.

Step 3: Understand Kew Gardens's Housing Stock

Kew Gardens offers something rare in Queens — pre-war co-op apartments under $350K with E/F express access to Manhattan in 28 minutes, zoned for the 8/10-rated PS 99.

Key question: Do you want a private 1-family home or a 2-family that generates rental income? In Kew Gardens, 2-family homes make up 15% of the market — meaning renters are plentiful and the math often works well for owner-occupant landlords.

Step 4: Hire a Local Buyer's Agent

A buyer's agent who specializes in Kew Gardens can alert you to listings before they hit Zillow, write competitive offers, and negotiate inspection credits. Crucially: in New York State, buyer's agent commissions are now negotiated directly with buyers — make sure you understand the agreement before signing.

Step 5: Make a Competitive Offer

In Kew Gardens, well-priced homes receive multiple offers within the first weekend. Strategies that work:

  • Escalation clause: Automatically outbid competing offers up to your ceiling
  • Larger earnest money deposit: 2–3% signals commitment to sellers
  • Flexible closing date: Sellers often need 60–90 days to coordinate a move
  • Pre-inspection: Some buyers waive the inspection contingency after a pre-inspection walk-through

Step 6: Home Inspection

Never skip a home inspection in Queens. Common issues in Kew Gardens's older housing stock include: aging boilers, knob-and-tube wiring (in pre-1950 homes), roof conditions, and basement moisture. Budget $500–$800 for a licensed NYC home inspector.

Step 7: Closing Costs in Queens

New York has some of the highest closing costs in the country. Buyers in Kew Gardens should budget:

  • Mortgage recording tax: 1.8–1.925% of loan amount
  • Title insurance: ~$1,500–$3,000
  • Attorney fee: $1,200–$2,500
  • NYC/NYS transfer taxes: paid by seller, but affects net price
  • Mansion Tax: 1% on purchases over $1M (paid by buyer)

Total buyer closing costs typically run 2–4% of purchase price in Queens.
