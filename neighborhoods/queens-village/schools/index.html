Understanding GreatSchools Ratings

GreatSchools rates schools 1–10 based on:

  • Test scores: State math and ELA proficiency rates
  • Academic progress: How much students improve year-over-year
  • Equity: How well the school serves all demographic groups

A rating of 6–7 is solid for New York City, where urban schools face resource challenges. Ratings of 8–10 are considered excellent and often reflect strong parent involvement and community support.

School District for Queens Village

Queens Village is served by NYC Department of Education. Queens has 14 community school districts; Queens Village falls within a district that also offers competitive high school admissions — including specialized high schools citywide (Stuyvesant, Bronx Science, etc.) which are accessible to any NYC student who qualifies via the SHSAT exam.

Private & Parochial Schools Near Queens Village

In addition to public schools, Queens Village and surrounding areas have access to Catholic, Jewish, and independent private schools. Families often consider Bishop Loughlin, St. Francis Prep, and other Queens-area parochial options depending on family preference and budget.

School Performance & Home Values

Research consistently shows that school quality correlates with home values. In Queens Village, homes zoned for higher-rated elementary schools often command a 5–12% premium over identical homes zoned for lower-rated schools. When comparing homes, always confirm the exact school zone — boundaries can change and don't always match what mapping tools show.

To verify your exact school zone: Use the NYC DOE Zone Finder with the exact property address.
