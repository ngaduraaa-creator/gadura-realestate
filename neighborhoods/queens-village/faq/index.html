Frequently Asked Questions About Queens Village, Queens NY

Common questions from buyers, sellers, and renters about Queens Village real estate

What is the average home price in Queens Village, Queens?
The median home price in Queens Village is $722,000 as of 2026-04, up +6.0% year-over-year. Prices range from $654,000 to $760,000 depending on property type and condition. Source: Rocket Homes / Zillow.
Is Queens Village a good place to buy a home?
Queens Village is considered an excellent value within Queens. Queens Village's Nassau County border location offers the best of both worlds: suburban Colonial and Cape Cod homes, LIRR commuter access, and NYC property taxes rather than Nassau's. With a 38-minute commute to Midtown Manhattan via LIRR Queens Village Station (Hempstead Branch), Queens Village offers significant lifestyle advantages at a fraction of the cost of closer-in neighborhoods.
What are property taxes like in Queens Village?
Property taxes in Queens Village are approximately $6,900/year for a median-priced home, reflecting an effective rate of 0.88%. This translates to roughly $575/month in tax escrow. Source: NYC Department of Finance FY2024–2025.
How long does it take to commute from Queens Village to Manhattan?
The average commute from Queens Village to Midtown Manhattan is approximately 38 minutes via LIRR Queens Village Station (Hempstead Branch). Additional transit options include Q1, Q27.
What are the best schools in Queens Village?
Public schools serving Queens Village include: PS 34 John Harvard (GreatSchools 6/10), PS 109 Jacob Blackwell (GreatSchools 6/10), Andrew Jackson High School (GreatSchools 4/10). For the most current ratings and to verify exact school zones for a specific property, use the GreatSchools.org tool.
Is Queens Village safe?
Queens Village is a residential neighborhood in Queens. Crime statistics for Queens are published by the NYPD and can be reviewed by precinct at NYPD CompStat. As with all neighborhoods, safety varies by specific block and time of day. We encourage prospective buyers to visit in person at different times.
What ZIP codes does Queens Village cover?
Queens Village is served by ZIP codes: 11427, 11428, 11429.
Are there investment properties available in Queens Village?
Yes. Queens Village's market includes a significant portion of 2-family homes — approximately 40% of the housing stock. These properties offer investors rental income potential of approximately $5,600/month combined. See our full Queens Village investment guide.
How do I get first-time buyer assistance in Queens Village?
First-time buyers in Queens Village may qualify for NYC HPD HomeFirst (up to $100,000 in down payment assistance), SONYMA below-market rate mortgages, and FHA loans with 3.5% down. See our complete first-time buyer guide for Queens Village.
What is Queens Village known for?
Queens Village's three ZIP codes (11427, 11428, 11429) each represent a distinct micro-neighborhood — Springfield Gardens, Queens Village proper, and Cambria Heights — each with slightly different home price profiles.
