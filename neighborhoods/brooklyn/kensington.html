About Kensington Real Estate

Kensington is one of Brooklyn's most genuinely multicultural neighborhoods, a quiet residential enclave tucked between Prospect Park to the north and Borough Park to the south. Often described as one of Brooklyn's best-kept secrets, Kensington is home to thriving Bangladeshi, Pakistani, Mexican, Uzbek, Chinese, and Orthodox Jewish communities that coexist in a vibrant patchwork of languages, cuisines, and traditions. The neighborhood's affordability relative to neighboring Park Slope and Windsor Terrace has drawn increasing attention from buyers seeking value near the park.

Kensington's housing stock consists primarily of pre-war apartment buildings, solid brick row houses, and detached single-family homes on leafy side streets. Many of the neighborhood's co-op buildings offer spacious layouts at price points well below comparable units in adjacent areas. For families, the proximity to Prospect Park's southern entrance -- with its playgrounds, Prospect Park Zoo, and LeFrak Center at Lakeside -- makes Kensington an especially attractive choice. The neighborhood rewards those who discover it with authentic community character and strong long-term value.

Kensington Real Estate Market Overview

Kensington remains one of the more affordable neighborhoods bordering Prospect Park. Co-ops are the dominant housing type, with one- and two-bedroom units typically ranging from $250,000 to $550,000. Single-family and two-family homes are available from $800,000 to $1.4 million, offering significantly more space than comparable prices deliver in Park Slope or Windsor Terrace. The neighborhood has seen steady appreciation as buyers discover its combination of park proximity, transit access, and multicultural character, making it a smart choice for both first-time buyers and long-term investors.

Living in Kensington

Kensington offers a genuinely diverse, family-friendly lifestyle with direct access to one of the greatest urban parks in the world and a food scene that rivals neighborhoods many times its size.

  • Parks & Recreation: Prospect Park's southern entrance is steps away, providing access to the Prospect Park Zoo, LeFrak Center at Lakeside for ice skating and roller skating, Prospect Park Lake, nature trails, and extensive playgrounds.
  • Shopping & Services: Church Avenue and McDonald Avenue are the main commercial corridors, featuring Bangladeshi and Pakistani grocery stores, Mexican panaderias, Uzbek markets, kosher shops, and everyday services. The neighborhood offers an incredibly diverse retail mix at affordable prices.
  • Transportation: The F and G trains stop at Fort Hamilton Parkway and Church Avenue, providing direct service to Manhattan. The B/Q trains are accessible at Prospect Park station. Multiple bus routes including the B35, B67, and B68 connect to surrounding areas.
  • Dining & Culture: Kensington is a food adventurer's paradise. Church Avenue and the surrounding blocks offer authentic Bangladeshi biryanis, Pakistani kebab houses, Uzbek plov restaurants, Mexican taquerias, and Chinese noodle shops -- all within walking distance of each other. The neighborhood's mosques, temples, and synagogues reflect its extraordinary cultural diversity.
