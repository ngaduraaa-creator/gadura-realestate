About Canarsie Real Estate

Canarsie is a sprawling residential neighborhood on Brooklyn's southeastern shore, bordered by Jamaica Bay and the Belt Parkway. Known for its suburban feel within city limits, Canarsie has long attracted families seeking spacious homes, driveways, backyards, and a quieter pace of life compared to the more urban parts of Brooklyn. The neighborhood is home to a large and vibrant Caribbean community, particularly Haitian, Jamaican, Trinidadian, and Guyanese families, whose cultural influence is woven into the local businesses, restaurants, churches, and annual festivals that give Canarsie its distinctive character.

The housing stock in Canarsie is predominantly single-family detached and semi-detached homes, many featuring private driveways, garages, and generous lot sizes that are rare in Brooklyn. Two-family homes are also common, offering owner-occupants the benefit of rental income. The neighborhood's proximity to Jamaica Bay, the Canarsie Pier, and the Gateway National Recreation Area gives residents access to waterfront recreation and open space that few Brooklyn neighborhoods can match. Rockaway Parkway serves as the main commercial corridor, with a mix of Caribbean eateries, bakeries, grocery stores, and service businesses.

Canarsie Real Estate Market Overview

Canarsie offers strong value for buyers seeking detached and semi-detached homes in Brooklyn. Single-family homes typically range from $600,000 to $1.1 million, with larger or newly renovated properties reaching $1.2 million or more. Two-family homes are priced between $800,000 and $1.4 million, making them attractive to owner-occupants who want to offset their mortgage with rental income. Multi-family properties with three or more units can reach $1.3 million to $1.8 million. Co-ops and condos, while less common in Canarsie, offer entry points starting in the $200,000 to $400,000 range. Rentals average $1,500 to $2,000 per month for one-bedroom apartments.

Living in Canarsie

Canarsie delivers a family-friendly, suburban-style lifestyle with the benefits of New York City services and public transit. The neighborhood is known for its strong community ties, active houses of worship, and cultural celebrations, particularly around Caribbean heritage events. Residents enjoy access to waterfront parks, good schools, and a commercial district that caters to daily needs. For families moving from apartment living in other parts of Brooklyn, Canarsie offers the space and privacy of homeownership at accessible price points.

  • Parks & Recreation: Canarsie Park and Canarsie Pier provide waterfront fishing, walking paths, playgrounds, and stunning views of Jamaica Bay. The Gateway National Recreation Area offers additional trails, bird-watching, and kayaking. Fresh Creek Nature Preserve adds a unique ecological area within the neighborhood.
  • Shopping & Services: Rockaway Parkway is the main shopping street with supermarkets, pharmacies, banks, and a variety of small businesses. The Gateway Center Mall in neighboring East New York provides big-box retail including Target, BJ's, and Burlington. Flatlands Avenue also offers additional commercial options.
  • Transportation: The L train terminates at Canarsie-Rockaway Parkway station, providing direct service to Manhattan's 14th Street corridor via Williamsburg and Bushwick. The B17, B42, B60, and B82 SBS bus routes connect Canarsie to surrounding neighborhoods and subway transfers. The Belt Parkway provides easy driving access to JFK Airport, Long Island, and the rest of Brooklyn.
  • Dining & Culture: Canarsie's dining scene is a celebration of Caribbean culture, with Haitian griot, Jamaican oxtail, Trinidadian roti, and Guyanese pepperpot available at dozens of restaurants and takeout spots along Rockaway Parkway and Flatlands Avenue. The neighborhood also hosts lively Caribbean carnival celebrations and cultural festivals throughout the year.
