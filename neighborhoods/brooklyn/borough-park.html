About Borough Park Real Estate

Borough Park is one of Brooklyn's largest and most distinctive neighborhoods, home to one of the biggest Orthodox Jewish communities outside of Israel. Located in the central-western part of the borough, Borough Park is defined by its close-knit family atmosphere, strong religious institutions, and a bustling commercial district centered along 13th Avenue and New Utrecht Avenue. The neighborhood's cultural identity is deeply woven into its daily rhythms, from the busy pre-Shabbat Friday shopping to the quiet, pedestrian-friendly streets on Saturday.

The real estate landscape in Borough Park is dominated by single-family and two-family brick row houses built primarily in the early-to-mid twentieth century. Many homes have been well-maintained or extensively renovated to accommodate large families, with finished basements, expanded kitchens, and additional living space. The neighborhood also features a growing number of new-construction condominiums and mixed-use buildings along its commercial corridors, reflecting steady demand for modern housing options in this tightly held market.

Borough Park Real Estate Market Overview

Borough Park's real estate market is driven by strong demand from families seeking spacious homes in an established community. Single-family homes typically sell between $900,000 and $1.6 million, while two-family homes range from $1.2 million to $2 million. Larger multi-family properties and new-construction homes can exceed $2.5 million. Condos in newer developments start around $400,000 for one-bedroom units and can reach $800,000 or more for larger layouts. The market moves quickly, with well-priced properties often receiving multiple offers within days of listing. Inventory tends to be limited, as many homeowners hold properties for decades.

Living in Borough Park

Life in Borough Park revolves around family, community, and tradition. The neighborhood is exceptionally safe and clean, with a strong civic culture that maintains the quality of its residential streets. Residents benefit from an extensive network of synagogues, yeshivas, and community organizations, as well as convenient access to shopping, healthcare, and public transit. Borough Park is also well-positioned geographically, sitting between Sunset Park, Bensonhurst, and Midwood with easy access to major Brooklyn thoroughfares.

  • Parks & Recreation: Borough Park Playground and the Dome Playground provide outdoor play space for children, while nearby Leif Ericson Park and Washington Cemetery add green buffers to the neighborhood. Sunset Park's massive green space and pool facility are just a short walk to the west.
  • Shopping & Services: 13th Avenue is the main commercial spine, offering an enormous selection of clothing stores, judaica shops, bakeries, grocery stores, electronics retailers, and household goods. New Utrecht Avenue adds additional shopping and dining options.
  • Transportation: The D train runs along New Utrecht Avenue with stops at 50th Street, 55th Street, and Fort Hamilton Parkway. The F and G trains are accessible at nearby Church Avenue. Multiple bus routes including the B9, B11, B35, and B70 provide cross-neighborhood connectivity.
  • Dining & Culture: Borough Park's restaurant scene features kosher delis, pizza shops, falafel stands, steakhouses, and bakeries known throughout Brooklyn for their challah, rugelach, and pastries. The neighborhood also hosts seasonal community events and celebrations that draw visitors from across the region.
