About Bensonhurst Real Estate

Bensonhurst is a sprawling residential neighborhood in southwestern Brooklyn that has long been one of the borough's most popular destinations for immigrant families seeking affordable homeownership and a strong community atmosphere. Historically an Italian-American enclave, Bensonhurst has evolved into one of the most ethnically diverse neighborhoods in New York City, with thriving Chinese, Russian, Pakistani, Mexican, and Central Asian communities that have transformed its commercial corridors into a global marketplace of flavors, languages, and cultures.

The neighborhood's housing stock is defined by neat rows of brick attached and semi-detached homes, many built in the 1920s through 1950s, along with a growing number of condominium conversions and new multi-family developments. 18th Avenue (known locally as Cristoforo Colombo Boulevard) and 86th Street are the primary shopping streets, packed with specialty grocers, dim sum houses, halal butchers, Italian delis, and bakeries that reflect the neighborhood's multicultural identity. Bensonhurst's quiet side streets and relatively affordable prices continue to draw first-time homebuyers and growing families.

Bensonhurst Real Estate Market Overview

Bensonhurst offers some of the most competitive pricing in Brooklyn for buyers seeking single-family and multi-family homes. One- and two-family brick homes typically range from $700,000 to $1.3 million, while multi-family investment properties with three or more units can reach $1.5 million to $2.2 million. Condos are available starting in the mid-$300,000s for one-bedroom units, with larger two- and three-bedroom condos ranging from $500,000 to $750,000. Co-ops provide an even more affordable entry point, with some units priced below $250,000. The rental market is steady, with one-bedroom apartments averaging $1,600 to $2,000 per month.

Living in Bensonhurst

Bensonhurst provides a quiet, family-oriented quality of life with outstanding access to food, shopping, and public transit. The neighborhood feels distinctly residential once you step off the main avenues, with well-kept homes, sidewalk gardens, and a slower pace that appeals to families and retirees alike. Its multicultural character means residents have access to authentic cuisines from around the world just steps from home.

  • Parks & Recreation: Bensonhurst Park offers ball fields, basketball courts, and playgrounds. Bath Beach Park and Calvert Vaux Park along Gravesend Bay provide waterfront green space, walking paths, and fishing areas.
  • Shopping & Services: 86th Street and 18th Avenue are major commercial hubs with supermarkets, banks, medical offices, and hundreds of small businesses. The neighborhood is also close to the Kings Plaza and Caesar's Bay shopping centers.
  • Transportation: The D train runs along the elevated tracks on 86th Street and New Utrecht Avenue with multiple stops throughout the neighborhood. The N train is also accessible nearby. Several bus lines including the B1, B4, B6, and B82 SBS connect residents to surrounding neighborhoods and transit hubs.
  • Dining & Culture: Bensonhurst is a food destination in its own right, famous for classic Italian bakeries and pizzerias alongside a booming Chinese restaurant scene on 86th Street, Uzbek and Pakistani eateries on 18th Avenue, and authentic Mexican taquerias scattered throughout.
