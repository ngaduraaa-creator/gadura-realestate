About Brooklyn Heights Real Estate

Brooklyn Heights holds the distinction of being New York City's first designated historic district, and it remains one of the most prestigious and sought-after residential neighborhoods in all of Brooklyn. Perched on a bluff overlooking the East River with iconic views of Lower Manhattan and the Statue of Liberty, Brooklyn Heights is renowned for its impeccably preserved Federal, Greek Revival, and Italianate row houses dating from the early 1800s. The tree-canopied streets, gas-lit lampposts, and elegant brownstone facades give the neighborhood an atmosphere of timeless refinement.

Montague Street serves as the neighborhood's charming main street, lined with restaurants, cafes, banks, and boutiques. The Brooklyn Heights Promenade, a cantilevered walkway stretching along the bluff's edge, offers some of the most photographed vistas in New York City and is a daily gathering spot for residents, joggers, and visitors. The neighborhood is home to prominent cultural and educational institutions, including St. Ann's School, the Brooklyn Historical Society, and numerous landmark churches that add to its architectural grandeur.

Brooklyn Heights Real Estate Market Overview

Brooklyn Heights is among the most expensive real estate markets in Brooklyn, reflecting its historic character, prime waterfront location, and proximity to Manhattan's Financial District. Townhouses and brownstones in the historic district rarely come to market, and when they do, prices typically range from $3 million to $10 million or more depending on size, condition, and original details. Co-op apartments are the most common housing type, with one-bedrooms starting around $600,000 and larger pre-war units with views commanding $1.5 million to $4 million. Condos in newer or converted buildings range from $800,000 for studios to well over $3 million for premium units. Rentals are competitive, with one-bedroom apartments averaging $2,800 to $3,800 per month.

Living in Brooklyn Heights

Brooklyn Heights offers an elevated quality of life that combines historic beauty with modern urban convenience. The neighborhood is exceptionally walkable, with a quiet residential feel just minutes from the bustle of Downtown Brooklyn and a quick subway ride to Manhattan. Families are drawn to the top-rated schools, safe streets, and abundant green space, while professionals appreciate the short commute and refined dining and shopping options along Montague and Henry Streets.

  • Parks & Recreation: The Brooklyn Heights Promenade provides stunning waterfront views and a peaceful walking path. Brooklyn Bridge Park, stretching along the waterfront below the bluff, offers 85 acres of playgrounds, sports facilities, kayaking, and seasonal events. Pierrepont Playground and Cadman Plaza Park add additional green space.
  • Shopping & Services: Montague Street is the primary retail corridor with bookstores, clothing boutiques, salons, and gourmet food shops. Atlantic Avenue, along the southern border, offers antique stores, Middle Eastern specialty shops, and home design showrooms.
  • Transportation: Brooklyn Heights is one of the best-connected neighborhoods in Brooklyn. The 2, 3, 4, 5 trains stop at Borough Hall and Clark Street. The A, C, and F trains are accessible at Jay Street-MetroTech. The R train stops at Court Street. The NYC Ferry also provides waterfront service from nearby DUMBO.
  • Dining & Culture: From classic neighborhood bistros and waterfront restaurants in Brooklyn Bridge Park to long-established Middle Eastern eateries on Atlantic Avenue, Brooklyn Heights offers refined dining in an intimate setting. Cultural attractions include the Brooklyn Historical Society, St. Ann & the Holy Trinity Church concerts, and proximity to BAM in neighboring Fort Greene.
