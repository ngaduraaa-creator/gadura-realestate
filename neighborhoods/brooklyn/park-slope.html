About Park Slope Real Estate

Park Slope is widely regarded as one of the finest residential neighborhoods in all of New York City. Defined by its stunning rows of Romanesque Revival, Queen Anne, and Renaissance Revival brownstones climbing the gentle slope toward Prospect Park, the neighborhood has been a coveted address since the late 19th century. The Park Slope Historic District, one of the largest in New York City, protects block after block of impeccably preserved townhouses that represent some of the best residential architecture in the country.

Today Park Slope is synonymous with family-friendly urban living at its best. The neighborhood's combination of world-class Prospect Park access, top-rated public schools including PS 321 and MS 51, a thriving independent retail scene along Fifth and Seventh Avenues, and an engaged civic community has made it a benchmark for Brooklyn real estate. The dining, cultural, and recreational amenities rival any neighborhood in the city, and the strong sense of community keeps families rooted here for generations.

Park Slope Real Estate Market Overview

Park Slope commands some of the highest residential prices in Brooklyn. Landmark brownstones and townhouses typically range from $2.5 million to $5 million or more, with prime properties on the blocks closest to Prospect Park reaching even higher. Condos range from $600,000 for one-bedrooms to over $2.5 million for larger units in boutique buildings. Co-ops offer a relative value, with one- and two-bedroom units ranging from $400,000 to $1.2 million. Competition is fierce, with desirable listings often receiving multiple offers within days. South Slope, the area below 9th Street, offers slightly more accessible pricing while sharing many of the neighborhood's best qualities.

Living in Park Slope

Park Slope delivers an unmatched quality of life anchored by Prospect Park, outstanding schools, walkable commercial streets, and a deeply engaged community that makes it one of the most desirable places to live in New York.

  • Parks & Recreation: Prospect Park is Park Slope's backyard -- 585 acres designed by Olmsted and Vaux featuring the Long Meadow, Prospect Park Lake, the Ravine, LeFrak Center at Lakeside, Prospect Park Zoo, and the Audubon Center. The neighborhood also enjoys the Old Stone House playground and JJ Byrne Park.
  • Shopping & Services: Fifth Avenue and Seventh Avenue are the twin commercial spines, packed with independent bookstores, clothing boutiques, toy shops, wine stores, gourmet grocers, and the legendary Park Slope Food Coop. The shopping experience here is overwhelmingly local and independent.
  • Transportation: Park Slope is exceptionally well-connected. The F and G trains at 4th Avenue/9th Street and 7th Avenue stations, the R train at Union Street and 9th Street, and the B/Q at 7th Avenue provide rapid access to Manhattan, Downtown Brooklyn, and beyond. Multiple bus routes supplement subway service.
  • Dining & Culture: The restaurant scene ranges from celebrated fine dining to beloved neighborhood institutions along Fifth and Seventh Avenues. The Brooklyn Conservatory of Music, community theater groups, and the vibrant farmers market at Grand Army Plaza add cultural richness. The Brooklyn Public Library's main branch at Grand Army Plaza is a landmark civic institution.
