About Dyker Heights Real Estate

Dyker Heights is one of Brooklyn's most prestigious residential neighborhoods, renowned for its spacious detached homes, manicured lawns, and deep Italian-American heritage that has defined its character for generations. Located in southwestern Brooklyn between Bay Ridge and Bensonhurst, Dyker Heights feels distinctly suburban with wide streets, generous lot sizes, and a quiet family-oriented atmosphere that stands apart from the density of central Brooklyn.

The neighborhood is perhaps best known nationally for its legendary Christmas lights displays, where homeowners transform entire blocks into spectacular holiday wonderlands that draw visitors from around the world every December. Beyond the seasonal fame, Dyker Heights offers year-round appeal with its well-maintained brick colonials, split-levels, and custom-built homes, many featuring private driveways, garages, and large backyards. The strong community pride visible in the neighborhood's pristine streets reflects the values of longtime residents and the new families who continue to be drawn to Dyker Heights for its safety, space, and traditional neighborhood feel.

Dyker Heights Real Estate Market Overview

Dyker Heights commands premium prices for Brooklyn's outer neighborhoods, reflecting the size and quality of its housing stock. Detached single-family homes typically range from $1 million to over $2.5 million, with larger custom-built properties and homes on prime blocks reaching even higher. Semi-attached and attached homes offer entry points starting around $700,000 to $1.2 million. Multi-family two- and three-family homes are available and popular among investors, generally priced from $1.2 million to $2 million. The market remains stable and desirable, supported by low crime rates, strong schools, and consistent demand from families seeking spacious homes with outdoor space while remaining in Brooklyn.

Living in Dyker Heights

Dyker Heights provides a family-centered suburban lifestyle within Brooklyn, with excellent parks, strong community institutions, and easy access to shopping and transit.

  • Parks & Recreation: Dyker Beach Park and Golf Course is the neighborhood's crown jewel, offering an 18-hole public golf course, tennis courts, baseball fields, and walking paths along the waterfront. The park provides sweeping views of the Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge and Lower New York Bay.
  • Shopping & Dining: 86th Street and 13th Avenue serve as the primary commercial corridors, lined with Italian bakeries, delis, pizzerias, Chinese restaurants, and family-owned businesses. Nearby Bay Ridge and Bensonhurst add depth with additional dining and retail options.
  • Transportation: The D train at 71st Street and the N train along 86th Street provide subway service to Manhattan. Express bus routes along the neighborhood's avenues offer direct commuter service to Midtown and Lower Manhattan, making Dyker Heights commuter-friendly despite its suburban feel.
  • Community & Traditions: Dyker Heights' Italian-American heritage is celebrated through neighborhood festivals, church events, and the world-famous Christmas lights tradition that transforms the area every holiday season. The strong sense of community and civic pride is evident in the neighborhood's beautifully maintained streets and homes.
