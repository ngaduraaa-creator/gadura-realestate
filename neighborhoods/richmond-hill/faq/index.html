Frequently Asked Questions About Richmond Hill, Queens NY

Common questions from buyers, sellers, and renters about Richmond Hill real estate

What is the average home price in Richmond Hill, Queens?
The median home price in Richmond Hill is $820,000 as of 2026-04, up +13.0% year-over-year. Prices range from $760,000 to $887,000 depending on property type and condition. Source: Redfin / Homes.com.
Is Richmond Hill a good place to buy a home?
Richmond Hill is considered an excellent value within Queens. Richmond Hill offers well-preserved Victorian architecture, Forest Park access, and a vibrant Liberty Avenue corridor — all at prices still below the Queens borough median. With a 42-minute commute to Midtown Manhattan via A Train (Ozone Park–Lefferts Blvd), Richmond Hill offers significant lifestyle advantages at a fraction of the cost of closer-in neighborhoods.
What are property taxes like in Richmond Hill?
Property taxes in Richmond Hill are approximately $7,200/year for a median-priced home, reflecting an effective rate of 0.88%. This translates to roughly $600/month in tax escrow. Source: NYC Department of Finance FY2024–2025.
How long does it take to commute from Richmond Hill to Manhattan?
The average commute from Richmond Hill to Midtown Manhattan is approximately 42 minutes via A Train (Ozone Park–Lefferts Blvd). Additional transit options include J/Z Train (Jamaica Ave), Q24.
What are the best schools in Richmond Hill?
Public schools serving Richmond Hill include: PS 66 North Shore Elementary (GreatSchools 6/10), PS 55 Norwood Avenue (GreatSchools 6/10), Richmond Hill High School (GreatSchools 4/10). For the most current ratings and to verify exact school zones for a specific property, use the GreatSchools.org tool.
Is Richmond Hill safe?
Richmond Hill is a residential neighborhood in Queens. Crime statistics for Queens are published by the NYPD and can be reviewed by precinct at NYPD CompStat. As with all neighborhoods, safety varies by specific block and time of day. We encourage prospective buyers to visit in person at different times.
What ZIP codes does Richmond Hill cover?
Richmond Hill is served by ZIP codes: 11418, 11419.
Are there investment properties available in Richmond Hill?
Yes. Richmond Hill's market includes a significant portion of 2-family homes — approximately 45% of the housing stock. These properties offer investors rental income potential of approximately $6,200/month combined. See our full Richmond Hill investment guide.
How do I get first-time buyer assistance in Richmond Hill?
First-time buyers in Richmond Hill may qualify for NYC HPD HomeFirst (up to $100,000 in down payment assistance), SONYMA below-market rate mortgages, and FHA loans with 3.5% down. See our complete first-time buyer guide for Richmond Hill.
What is Richmond Hill known for?
Richmond Hill's Liberty Avenue is home to one of the largest concentrations of Indo-Caribbean and Punjabi businesses outside of South Asia — including grocery stores, temples, restaurants, and professional services serving dozens of communities.
