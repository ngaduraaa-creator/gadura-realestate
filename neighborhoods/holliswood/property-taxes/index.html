How Property Taxes Work in Holliswood

Holliswood is located in Queens County, New York City. NYC calculates property taxes based on the assessed value of your home, which is different from market value.

In New York City, Class 1 residential properties (1–3 family homes) are assessed at 6% of market value, then taxed at NYC's composite Class 1 rate. Property Tax Exemptions Available in Holliswood

New York City offers several exemptions that can significantly reduce your tax bill:

  • STAR (School Tax Relief): Basic STAR available to homeowners earning under $500K/year; Enhanced STAR for seniors 65+ earning under $98,700. Saves $300–$1,500/year.
  • Veterans Exemption: Eligible veterans can receive a reduction in assessed value
  • Senior Citizen Exemption: Income-qualified seniors 65+ may receive up to 50% assessment reduction
  • Disabled Persons Exemption: For qualifying disabled homeowners

Apply for STAR at NYS Department of Taxation.

Appealing Your Assessment

If you believe your home is over-assessed, you have the right to appeal. In New York City:

  • File with NYC Tax Commission by March 15 (Class 1 properties)
  • Provide evidence of market value: recent appraisal or comparable sales
  • Applications available at NYC Tax Commission

Property Tax vs. Neighboring Municipalities

Holliswood's effective tax rate of 0.88% compares favorably to neighboring areas. NYC's tax structure generally results in lower effective rates than suburban Nassau/Suffolk counties, though NYC also imposes additional local taxes.
