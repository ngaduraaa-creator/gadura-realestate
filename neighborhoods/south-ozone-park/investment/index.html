Why South Ozone Park Is Popular With Investors

2-family homes make up 55% of South Ozone Park's housing stock, giving investors a large selection of income-producing properties. The rental market here is strong: proximity to major transit lines (including A Train (Howard Beach–JFK, nearby)) keeps vacancy rates low.

Property taxes in South Ozone Park run approximately $6,900/year (0.88% effective rate), which is among the lowest in Queens. Source: NYC Department of Finance FY2024–2025.

What to Look for in a South Ozone Park Investment Property

  • Legal 2-family status: Verify with NYC DOF — only legal 2-family homes can be legally rented as two units
  • Separate utilities: Metered separately means lower landlord operating costs
  • Updated mechanicals: New boiler and roof reduce capex in years 1–5
  • Block quality: Corner lots and tree-lined blocks command 5–8% rent premiums
  • Rent stabilization status: Queens 2-family homes under 6 units are generally market-rate

Financing Investment Properties in South Ozone Park

Investment property financing (non-owner-occupied) typically requires:

  • 25–30% down payment
  • Minimum 680 credit score (720+ for best rates)
  • 6 months reserves after closing
  • Debt-to-income ratio under 43–45%

Owner-occupied 2-family (you live in one unit, rent the other): FHA loans allow 3.5% down and count 75% of rental income toward qualifying income — making owner-occupied 2-family one of the best wealth-building strategies for first-time buyers in Queens.

Market Outlook for South Ozone Park Investors

South Ozone Park has seen +6.4% appreciation over the past year. With limited new construction and strong demand from renters and owner-occupants alike, South Ozone Park investment properties are expected to remain competitive in 2026.
