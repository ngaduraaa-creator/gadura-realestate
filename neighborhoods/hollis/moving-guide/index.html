What Hollis Is Like

Hollis leads SE Queens in single-family home concentration — 43% of properties are detached 1-family homes on tree-lined blocks near the Nassau border.

Hollis holds a special place in American music history — the neighborhood is the birthplace of hip-hop legends Run-DMC, LL Cool J, and Ja Rule, all of whom grew up on Hollis Avenue.

Getting Around Hollis

Transit options: LIRR Hollis Station (Hempstead Branch), Q2, Q3. Average commute to Manhattan: 38 minutes.

Practical Moving Checklist for Hollis

  1. Update your address: USPS change of address, DMV (NY requires update within 10 days of moving), IRS, employer, bank, voter registration
  2. Utilities setup: ConEd for electricity, National Grid for gas; call 2 weeks before move-in
  3. Sanitation: NYC Sanitation handles trash/recycling — check your pickup days at NYC DSNY
  4. Schools: Register at your local district office with proof of address; school year starts early September
  5. Library card: Queens Public Library has branches throughout Hollis — free access to books, internet, and community programs
  6. Community boards: Queens has 14 community boards; look up yours to stay informed on local issues
