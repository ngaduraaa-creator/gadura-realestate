Best Neighborhoods for the Pakistani Community in New York

Jackson Heights, Queens

The cultural heart of Pakistani New York. The 74th Street corridor features Pakistani restaurants serving nihari, haleem, and biryani, along with halal butchers, clothing shops, and jewelers. The neighborhood comes alive during Chand Raat celebrations before Eid. Excellent transit via the 7 train. A mix of co-ops, condos, and multi-family homes.

Midwood & Coney Island Ave, Brooklyn

Brooklyn's primary Pakistani corridor runs along Coney Island Avenue from Midwood through Kensington. The stretch is lined with Pakistani and South Asian restaurants, halal meat markets, Islamic bookstores, and community organizations. Several mosques serve the area. Affordable apartments and multi-family homes make this a popular entry point for new families.

Ozone Park & South Ozone Park

A growing Pakistani community with affordable single-family and two-family homes. Close to JFK Airport and the A train, making it convenient for families with international connections. Several mosques serve the area, and halal restaurants and grocery stores are increasingly common along the main commercial corridors.

Kensington, Brooklyn

Adjacent to the Coney Island Avenue corridor, Kensington has a significant Pakistani and Bangladeshi population. Church Avenue and surrounding streets feature South Asian businesses, halal eateries, and community centers. More affordable than many Brooklyn neighborhoods with good transit access via the F and G trains.

Jamaica, Queens

A diverse transportation hub with a growing Pakistani presence. Offers a wide range of housing options from detached homes to new developments. Multiple mosques serve the area. Proximity to the LIRR, AirTrain, and several subway lines makes it ideal for commuters and families who travel frequently to Pakistan.

Hicksville & New Hyde Park, Long Island

Long Island communities with growing Pakistani populations drawn by excellent school districts, suburban homes with larger lots, and proximity to mosques and South Asian businesses. Hicksville's Broadway corridor features Pakistani and Indian restaurants and grocery stores. Good LIRR access to Manhattan.

Pakistani Community Resources in New York

New York's Pakistani community is supported by a rich network of mosques, Islamic centers, community organizations, and cultural institutions. Mosques in Jackson Heights, Ozone Park, Midwood, and other neighborhoods provide not just a place for prayer but a hub for community life, offering religious education, youth programs, marriage services, and social gatherings. The community celebrates Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha with large gatherings, special prayers, and festive bazaars.

Pakistani grocery stores, halal butchers, and restaurants are abundant in established neighborhoods, providing access to familiar foods from every region of Pakistan. Community organizations offer immigration assistance, language support, professional networking, and cultural events that keep families connected to their heritage while building new lives in New York.
