Best Neighborhoods for the Hispanic & Latino Community in New York

Jackson Heights, Queens

One of the most diverse neighborhoods in the world, Jackson Heights has a massive Latin American community with Colombian, Ecuadorian, and Mexican businesses lining Roosevelt Avenue. The neighborhood features Latin American bakeries, restaurants, remittance offices, and community organizations. Excellent transit via the 7 train. Co-ops, condos, and multi-family homes available.

Corona, Queens

A deeply rooted Latino neighborhood with a strong Mexican, Ecuadorian, and Colombian presence. Junction Boulevard and Roosevelt Avenue serve as the commercial heart, with taquerias, panaderias, and Latin grocery stores on every block. Affordable multi-family homes and proximity to Flushing Meadows-Corona Park make it ideal for families.

Elmhurst, Queens

A melting pot with a significant Hispanic population alongside Asian and South Asian communities. Broadway and Queens Boulevard feature Latin American restaurants and businesses. Offers a range of housing from co-ops to multi-family homes at relatively accessible price points. Excellent transit connections.

Woodside, Queens

A family-friendly neighborhood with a growing Latino community, particularly from Mexico and Colombia. Good public schools, tree-lined residential streets, and convenient access to the 7 train. A mix of attached homes, co-ops, and multi-family properties at various price points.

Sunset Park, Brooklyn

Brooklyn's vibrant Mexican and Latin American hub centered along Fifth Avenue. The neighborhood features authentic taquerias, bakeries, grocery stores, and community organizations. Offers stunning views of the Manhattan skyline from Sunset Park. A mix of row houses, multi-family homes, and new developments at accessible prices.

Bushwick, Brooklyn

A historically Dominican and Puerto Rican neighborhood with a thriving arts and culture scene. Knickerbocker Avenue and Broadway feature Latin American businesses, bodegas, and restaurants. The area offers multi-family homes and townhouses with strong investment potential and growing property values.

East New York, Brooklyn

One of Brooklyn's most affordable neighborhoods with a significant Latino population. Offers detached homes, multi-family properties, and new development opportunities. Strong community organizations, churches, and cultural centers serve the Hispanic community. Good transit via the L and A/C trains.

Hispanic & Latino Community Resources in New York

New York's Hispanic and Latino community is supported by a powerful network of churches, community organizations, cultural institutions, and business associations. Catholic parishes and evangelical churches serve as community anchors across Queens and Brooklyn, hosting not just religious services but community events, youth programs, and social services. Annual celebrations like Hispanic Heritage Month, Cinco de Mayo, Dominican Day Parade, and Puerto Rican Day Parade bring together millions in celebration of Latino culture.

Latin American grocery stores, bakeries, and restaurants are the lifeblood of neighborhoods like Corona, Jackson Heights, and Sunset Park, providing access to familiar foods and a taste of home. Community organizations offer immigration assistance, legal services, English language classes, job training, and youth programming. Living in an established Latino neighborhood means access to a support system that helps families thrive.
