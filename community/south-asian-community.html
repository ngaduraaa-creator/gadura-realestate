Best Neighborhoods for South Asian Families in New York

Jackson Heights, Queens

The undisputed capital of South Asian New York. The 74th Street corridor features Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and Nepali businesses side by side. Sari shops, jewelry stores, halal butchers, sweet shops, and restaurants serving cuisine from every region of the subcontinent. Excellent transit via the 7 train.

Ozone Park & South Ozone Park

A rapidly growing South Asian enclave with affordable single-family and multi-family homes. Strong Indian, Pakistani, and Bangladeshi presence. Close to JFK Airport and the A train. Ideal for multi-generational families and real estate investors seeking solid rental returns.

Flushing, Queens

An increasingly popular destination for South Asian families, offering newer condo developments and established residential streets. Proximity to Hindu temples and South Asian cultural organizations. Strong public schools and convenient LIRR access to Manhattan.

Kensington, Brooklyn

A historically diverse neighborhood with a significant Bangladeshi and Pakistani population. Church Avenue and the surrounding streets feature South Asian grocery stores, halal restaurants, and community organizations. Affordable housing options compared to neighboring areas.

Hicksville, Long Island

The heart of Long Island's South Asian community. Broadway in Hicksville is lined with Indian and Pakistani restaurants, grocery stores, and professional offices. Suburban homes with generous lot sizes, top-rated school districts, and a deeply rooted desi community.

New Hyde Park & Floral Park

Family-friendly communities on the Queens-Nassau border with excellent schools and well-established South Asian populations. A mix of Cape Cod, Colonial, and split-level homes. Convenient LIRR access and proximity to temples, mosques, and cultural organizations.

Jamaica & Richmond Hill, Queens

Diverse neighborhoods with growing South Asian communities, particularly from the Indo-Caribbean diaspora. A wide range of housing options from detached homes to new developments. Richmond Hill's Liberty Avenue corridor features a unique blend of South Asian and Caribbean culture.

South Asian Community Resources in New York

New York's South Asian community is supported by an extensive network of religious institutions, cultural organizations, and community services. Hindu mandirs, Sikh gurudwaras, mosques, Buddhist viharas, and Jain centers serve communities across Queens, Brooklyn, and Long Island. Annual celebrations like Diwali, Eid, Holi, Vesak, and Vaisakhi bring together thousands of families each year.

South Asian grocery stores and restaurants are abundant in neighborhoods like Jackson Heights, Hicksville, Ozone Park, and Kensington, providing access to familiar foods and ingredients from every region of the subcontinent. Community organizations offer support for new immigrants, language classes, youth programs, professional networking, and cultural education. Living in an established South Asian neighborhood means staying connected to your roots while building a future in New York.

What neighborhoods have the largest South Asian populations in Queens?

Jackson Heights is the cultural epicenter, with a massive concentration of South Asian businesses along 74th Street and Roosevelt Avenue serving Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, and Nepali communities. Ozone Park and South Ozone Park have rapidly growing South Asian populations. Flushing and Jamaica also offer strong communities with newer housing options.

Can Nitin help South Asian families find multi-generational homes?

Absolutely. Multi-generational living is central to South Asian culture across Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi, Sri Lankan, and Nepali families. Nitin specializes in finding homes with in-law suites, separate entrances, multiple kitchens, and enough bedrooms for grandparents, parents, and children to live together comfortably.
