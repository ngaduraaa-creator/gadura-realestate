Neighborhood Overview

What makes Rockville Centre 11570 distinctive

Zip code 11570 covers the Village of Rockville Centre, an incorporated village in the Town of Hempstead, Nassau County. Rockville Centre is one of Long Island’s most established and sought-after suburban communities, with a vibrant walkable village center along North Village Avenue and Merrick Road — restaurants, independent shops, a weekly farmers market, and a consistent community calendar that gives the village genuine year-round character. The community is predominantly white-collar families who value school quality, LIRR commute access, and a village lifestyle that feels authentically local rather than chain-retail suburban.

Housing in Rockville Centre is primarily postwar Colonials, Tudors, Cape Cods, and some Victorian-era homes on the older blocks near the village center, typically on 50×100 to 80×150 lots. The Rockville Centre Union Free School District is consistently ranked among the top 15 districts in Nassau County, with South Side High School known for strong academic programs and competitive athletics. The LIRR Long Beach and West Hempstead branches both serve Rockville Centre station, providing direct service to Penn Station in approximately 35 minutes and to Atlantic Terminal in Brooklyn. Annual property taxes in Rockville Centre typically run $15,000–$25,000. Inventory is historically tight; well-priced homes frequently receive multiple offers within a week. Pre-approval and decisive action are essential for buyers in this market.

Common Questions

FAQ — Buying & selling in 11570 Rockville Centre

How competitive is the Rockville Centre real estate market?

Rockville Centre is one of Nassau County’s most competitive markets. Inventory is consistently below demand, and well-priced homes regularly receive multiple offers within 5–10 days of listing. Buyers should be pre-approved before looking and ready to move quickly. Waiving minor contingencies or offering flexible closing dates can make the difference in a competitive situation.

What are the schools like in Rockville Centre 11570?

The Rockville Centre Union Free School District is consistently ranked among Nassau County’s top districts. South Side High School has a strong academic record, Advanced Placement program, and competitive athletics. The district is relatively small, allowing for strong per-pupil resources. School quality is one of the primary drivers of real estate demand in 11570.

What is the typical price for a home in Rockville Centre?

In 2024–2025, homes in Rockville Centre 11570 typically sell in the $800,000–$1.3 million range. Smaller Cape Cods and entry-level Colonials start around $750,000–$850,000; updated larger Colonials and Tudors trade from $1 million to $1.3 million. Properties on premium blocks near the village center or with exceptional renovations occasionally exceed $1.5 million.

What is the LIRR commute from Rockville Centre to Manhattan?

Rockville Centre is served by both the Long Beach and West Hempstead LIRR branches, providing direct service to Penn Station in approximately 35–42 minutes. Peak trains run frequently during rush hour. The station is within walking distance of much of the village, making car-free commuting feasible for many residents.
