About West Ozone Park (11416)

West Ozone Park is a tightly knit, predominantly working-class neighborhood in southern Queens, bordered by Woodhaven Boulevard to the west, Liberty Avenue to the north, and Linden Boulevard to the south. The housing stock here is almost entirely detached and semi-detached one- and two-family homes built between the 1920s and 1960s — brick and frame construction on standard 25×100 lots. These homes are substantial: most two-families run 1,800–2,400 sq ft with full basements and private driveways, making them attractive to multi-generational families who can offset their mortgage with rental income from the second unit.

The neighborhood draws a deeply diverse population — South Asian, Caribbean, and Latino families predominate — and has held its value well through market cycles because demand consistently outpaces supply. Liberty Avenue is the commercial spine, lined with South Asian and Indo-Caribbean businesses, halal butchers, sari shops, and bakeries that give the neighborhood its distinct character. PS 97, IS 202, and John Adams High School serve the area. Aqueduct Racetrack and the Ozone Park sports complex are nearby recreation anchors.
