The 1031 Exchange Timeline — What Investors Get Wrong

Under IRC §1031, like-kind exchanges of real property held for investment or productive use in a trade or business can defer federal capital-gains tax [1]. The post-2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act limited §1031 to real property only; personal property no longer qualifies.

  • Day 0: Closing on the relinquished property. Proceeds go to a qualified intermediary — not to you. Touching the cash breaks the exchange.
  • Day 45: Identification deadline. You must identify replacement property in writing under one of three rules: Three-Property Rule, 200% Rule, or 95% Rule.
  • Day 180: Closing deadline. You must close on identified replacement property (or file your tax return, whichever is earlier).

Miss either deadline and the deferral fails. There are no extensions except narrow federally-declared disaster relief.

What Qualifies as Like-Kind in NY

  • Multi-family residential (2–4 unit and 5+ unit)
  • Mixed-use (commercial ground floor + residential above) common on Crossbay Boulevard, Liberty Avenue, Jamaica Avenue, Hillside Avenue
  • Small office and retail
  • Raw land held for investment
  • Fractional interests via Delaware Statutory Trust (DST) where structured properly

What does not qualify: your primary residence, property held primarily for resale (fix-and-flip), or stocks/securities.
