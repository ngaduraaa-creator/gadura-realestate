# Dialpad AI Playbook: Inbound Buyer Lead Call

**Playbook Name:** Buyer Lead Qualification
**Trigger:** Auto-activate when caller mentions "buy," "looking for," "house," "apartment," "home," "condo," "property," "move to"
**Agent:** Nitin Gadura, Gadura Real Estate LLC
**Markets:** Queens, Long Island (Nassau County), Brooklyn

---

## How This Playbook Works

When an inbound call is detected as a potential buyer inquiry, Dialpad displays real-time prompt cards on Nitin's screen during the live call. Each card contains the script and talking points for that stage of the conversation. Cards advance automatically based on AI detection of conversation topics, or Nitin can manually advance them.

---

## Card 1: Professional Greeting

**Trigger:** Call begins
**Display Time:** First 30 seconds

### Script

> "Thank you for calling Gadura Real Estate, this is Nitin Gadura speaking. How can I help you today?"

### Prompt Notes
- Smile while speaking -- it changes your tone
- Use the caller's name once they provide it: "Great to hear from you, [Name]"
- If they say they found you online: "Glad our website was helpful! Which page caught your attention?"
- If referral: "That's wonderful -- [referrer name] is great. How do you know them?"

---

## Card 2: Initial Discovery

**Trigger:** After greeting exchange
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script

> "I'd love to help you find the right property. Let me ask a few quick questions so I can match you with the best options. Where are you currently located?"

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask
1. "Are you currently renting or do you own?"
2. "What's prompting your search right now?" (Listen for: lease ending, growing family, investment, relocating, first-time buyer)
3. "How long have you been looking?"
4. "Have you been working with another agent?" (If yes: "No problem at all -- are you under a buyer's agreement with them?")

### Key Phrases to Listen For
- "First time" -- flag as first-time buyer, will need extra guidance
- "Investment" -- shift to investment property questions
- "Relocating" -- ask where from, timeline is usually tight
- "Parents" or "family" -- multi-generational living, suggest two-family homes

---

## Card 3: Financial Qualification

**Trigger:** AI detects discussion moving past initial discovery, or manually advance
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script

> "To make sure I'm showing you properties that are the right fit, can I ask about your financial situation? Have you spoken with a mortgage lender or gotten pre-approved yet?"

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask
1. **Pre-approval status:**
   - "Have you been pre-approved for a mortgage?"
   - If yes: "Great -- what amount were you approved for, and which lender are you working with?"
   - If no: "That's perfectly fine -- I work with several excellent mortgage brokers who specialize in [first-time buyers / investment properties / the Queens market]. I'd recommend getting pre-approved first so you know exactly what you can afford and sellers take your offer seriously. Want me to connect you with one?"
2. **Budget range:**
   - "What price range are you comfortable with?"
   - If hesitant: "For Queens and Nassau County, most [condos/co-ops] start around $300K-$500K, single-family homes are typically $500K-$900K, and two-family homes run $700K-$1.2M. Does any of that range feel right?"
   - Budget ranges for the market:
     - Co-ops: $250K - $450K
     - Condos: $350K - $650K
     - Single-family: $500K - $900K
     - Two-family: $700K - $1.2M
     - Multi-family (3-4 unit): $900K - $2M
3. **Down payment:**
   - "Do you have a sense of your down payment amount?"
   - For first-time buyers: "There are some great programs for first-time buyers in New York -- FHA loans with as little as 3.5% down, and SONYMA programs with down payment assistance. I can walk you through those."
4. **Cash buyer check:**
   - If budget is high or they mention cash: "Will this be a cash purchase or financed?"

### Red Flags to Note
- No pre-approval and no plan to get one -- offer lender referral, but this lead needs nurturing
- Budget significantly mismatched to desired area -- educate gently on market pricing
- Already under buyer agreement with another agent -- respect the agreement, offer to help after it expires

## Card 4: Property Requirements

**Trigger:** After financial qualification
**Display Time:** 3-4 minutes

### Script

> "Perfect. Now let's talk about what you're looking for in a home. What type of property are you interested in?"

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask

**Property Type:**
- "Are you looking for a condo, co-op, single-family house, two-family, or multi-family?"
- If unsure: "A lot of buyers in Queens love two-family homes -- you can live in one unit and rent the other to help cover your mortgage. That's a really popular strategy in our area."

**Size and Layout:**
- "How many bedrooms do you need?"
- "How many bathrooms?"
- "Do you need a garage or driveway?"
- "Is a backyard important to you?"
- "Any preference on layout -- do you want an eat-in kitchen, open floor plan, separate dining room?"

**Condition:**
- "Are you open to a home that needs some work, or do you want move-in ready?"
- "Would you consider a gut renovation if the price is right?"

**Must-Haves vs. Nice-to-Haves:**
- "If you could only pick three things that are absolute must-haves, what would they be?"
- "And what would be on your wish list but you could live without?"

**Special Requirements:**
- "Do you need an in-law suite or separate entrance?" (common for multi-generational families)
- "Any accessibility needs -- first-floor bedroom, elevator building, no stairs?"
- "Do you need a home office or workspace?"

## Card 5: Neighborhood and Location

**Trigger:** After property requirements
**Display Time:** 3-4 minutes

### Script

> "Location is everything in real estate. What neighborhoods or areas are you most interested in?"

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask

**Neighborhood Preference:**
- "Do you have specific neighborhoods in mind?"
- If vague: "Let me help narrow it down. Here are some popular areas I specialize in:"
  - **Howard Beach** -- quiet, residential, waterfront feel, strong community
  - **Ozone Park** -- diverse, affordable, excellent transit access
  - **Richmond Hill** -- vibrant community, great food scene, two-family homes
  - **South Ozone Park** -- family-oriented, close to JFK, good value
  - **Jamaica** -- urban, transit hub, investment opportunities
  - **Woodhaven** -- tree-lined streets, parks, suburban feel in the city
  - **Nassau County** -- suburban, more space, good schools, slightly higher prices

**Commute:**
- "Where do you work? What's your commute situation?"
- "Do you drive or take public transit?"
- "How long of a commute is acceptable?"

**Schools:**
- "Do you have children or plan to? School district is a big factor."
- "Would you like me to send you school rating information for areas you're interested in?"

## Card 6: Community-Specific Questions

**Trigger:** AI detects cultural or community references, or manually advance when appropriate
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script

> "One thing I pride myself on is knowing the communities in Queens and Long Island inside and out. I work with families from many different backgrounds, and I want to make sure the neighborhood feels like home for you."

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask (Use Naturally, Not as a Checklist)

**Language and Cultural Fit:**
- "Is language preference important for the neighborhood? I can help you find areas with strong [Hindi/Urdu/Bengali/Spanish/Creole/Caribbean] speaking communities."
- "Are you looking to be near a particular community -- Indian, Guyanese, Bengali, Caribbean, Hispanic?"

**Proximity to Cultural Resources:**
- "Is being close to a particular house of worship important to you? There are several [temples/mandirs/mosques/gurdwaras/churches] in the neighborhoods I work in."
- "Do you want to be near particular grocery stores or markets? For example, Patel Brothers, Apna Bazar, Caribbean markets?"
- "Are there community organizations or cultural centers you'd want to be near?"

**Specific Community Knowledge to Share:**
- **Indian community:** Richmond Hill (Little Guyana/Punjab), Ozone Park, South Ozone Park, Hicksville (LI), Floral Park (LI)
- **Guyanese community:** Richmond Hill (Liberty Avenue), South Ozone Park, Ozone Park
- **Bengali community:** Jamaica, Ozone Park, Jackson Heights, Hamtramck area (mention Kensington)
- **Caribbean community:** Jamaica, Cambria Heights, Springfield Gardens, Laurelton
- **Hispanic community:** Jackson Heights, Corona, Woodhaven, Jamaica
- **Black community:** Jamaica, Cambria Heights, St. Albans, Laurelton, Rosedale

**Cultural Fit Reassurance:**
- "I work with a lot of families from the [X] community and I understand what's important -- being near family, having the right schools, access to cultural resources. I'll make sure we find the right fit."

## Card 7: Appointment Setting

**Trigger:** After qualification is complete (budget, property type, and neighborhood discussed)
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script -- For Ready Buyers

> "Based on everything you've told me, I already have some properties in mind that could be a great fit. The best next step would be to set up a time to see them in person. When are you available this week?"

### Script -- For Early-Stage Buyers

> "It sounds like you're in the early stages, which is great -- you're being smart about this. Here's what I'd suggest: Let me set up a custom search for you so you get new listings in [neighborhood] the moment they hit the market. Then let's schedule a quick meeting -- even 20 minutes over coffee -- so I can learn more about what you want and show you what's available. How does that sound?"

### Prompt Notes
- Always propose specific times: "I have openings Tuesday at 2 PM or Wednesday at 10 AM -- which works better?"
- For showings: "I usually schedule 3-4 properties in one trip so we make the most of your time."
- If they hesitate: "No pressure at all -- this is just a chance for you to see what's out there. No obligation, and I'll give you my honest opinion on every property."
- Confirm appointment details: date, time, meeting location, properties to see
- Ask: "Who else will be joining us? A spouse, partner, family member? I want to make sure everyone's questions get answered."

## Card 8: Objection Handling

**Trigger:** AI detects objection phrases
**Display Time:** Until resolved

### Objection: "What's your commission?" / "How do agents get paid?"

> "Great question. With the recent changes in real estate, I want to be completely transparent. As a buyer, here's how it works: my commission is typically covered by the seller as part of the transaction. Before we start looking at homes together, we'll sign a buyer representation agreement that spells out exactly what I'll do for you and how compensation works. There are zero surprises. My job is to protect your interests, negotiate the best price, and make the process smooth. Does that make sense?"

### Objection: "I can just use Zillow/Redfin/an app"

> "Absolutely, those are great tools for browsing -- I actually encourage my clients to use them. Here's what I add on top of that: I know about listings before they hit those sites, I know the neighborhoods block by block, and I negotiate on your behalf. Last month, I saved a buyer $30K on a property in [neighborhood] because I knew the seller's situation. Plus, I handle the entire process -- inspections, attorneys, mortgage coordination, closing -- so you don't have to figure it all out yourself. The apps show you houses; I help you actually get the right one at the right price."

### Objection: "We're just browsing" / "Not ready yet"

> "That's totally fine -- most of my clients start by exploring. Here's what I'd suggest: let me set you up with a custom property search based on what you've told me. You'll get new listings emailed to you as soon as they come on the market. When something catches your eye, just call or text me and we'll go see it. No pressure, no commitment. How does that sound?"

### Objection: "We want to wait for prices to drop"

> "I completely understand that thinking. Here's what I'm seeing in the Queens and Long Island market right now: [share current market data -- inventory levels, average days on market, price trends]. Historically, waiting has cost buyers more in this area because prices have [trended up / remained stable] and interest rates [current situation]. But I respect your timeline -- let me keep you informed on market trends so you can make the best decision for your family. Can I add you to my market update list?"

### Objection: "I have a friend/family member who's an agent"

> "I totally respect that -- relationships matter. If you're committed to working with them, I wish you the best. But if you ever want a second opinion or if things don't work out, I'm here. Can I at least send you my card and some market info for the areas you mentioned? That way you have a local resource."

## Card 9: Closing and Next Steps

**Trigger:** Conversation winding down
**Display Time:** Until call ends

### Script -- Appointment Set

> "Perfect, so I've got you down for [day] at [time]. Here's what I'll do before we meet: I'll pull together the best listings in [neighborhood] that match your criteria, run comparable sales so you know what properties are selling for, and have everything ready for you. I'll send you a confirmation text right after we hang up with my contact info. Is there anything else I can help with before then?"

### Script -- No Appointment (Nurture Lead)

> "I'm glad we connected, [Name]. Here's what happens next: I'm going to set up that custom property search for you right now. You'll start getting listings in your email. I'll also send you a quick text with my direct number so you can reach me anytime -- call, text, whatever's easiest. And I'll check in with you in about [appropriate timeframe] to see how the search is going. Sound good?"

### Prompt Notes
- Always confirm their preferred contact method: call, text, or email
- Send a text immediately after the call with: name, title, company, and next step
- Add to follow-up calendar:
  - Hot lead (appointment set): follow up same day with confirmation
  - Warm lead (interested, no appointment): follow up in 2-3 days
  - Cool lead (just browsing): follow up in 1-2 weeks with market update
  - Future buyer (not ready): add to monthly market update list

### Within 24 Hours
- [ ] Send property matches via email
- [ ] Set up automated listing alerts if available
- [ ] Calendar the follow-up date
- [ ] If pre-approval needed: send lender referral

### Follow-Up Cadence
| Lead Temperature | First Follow-Up | Cadence |
|-----------------|----------------|---------|
| Hot (appointment set) | Same day confirmation | Every 2-3 days until showing |
| Warm (interested) | 2-3 days | Weekly for 4 weeks |
| Cool (browsing) | 1 week | Bi-weekly for 8 weeks |
| Future (not ready) | 2 weeks | Monthly market updates |
