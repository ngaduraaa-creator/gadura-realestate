# Dialpad AI Scorecard: Gadura Real Estate Call Quality

**Scorecard Name:** Gadura Real Estate Lead Call Quality
**Applied To:** All inbound calls over 60 seconds
**Scoring:** AI auto-scores each criterion as Pass (1 point) or Fail (0 points)
**Minimum Passing Score:** 7 out of 10
**Review Schedule:** Weekly (Monday morning analytics review)

---

## The 10-Item Scorecard

### Item 1: Professional Greeting (Introduced Self and Company)

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent stated their name ("This is Nitin Gadura" or "Nitin speaking")
- Agent stated the company name ("Gadura Real Estate")
- Greeting was warm and professional (not rushed or abrupt)

**Pass Examples:**
- "Thank you for calling Gadura Real Estate, this is Nitin Gadura."
- "Gadura Real Estate, Nitin speaking, how can I help you?"
- "Hi, this is Nitin from Gadura Real Estate."

**Fail Examples:**
- "Hello?" (no name or company)
- "Yeah, what's up?" (unprofessional)
- Jumping straight to business without greeting

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 2: Asked Qualifying Question (Budget/Pre-Approval)

**What AI Listens For:**
- For buyers: Asked about budget range, pre-approval status, or financing
- For sellers: Asked about price expectations or what they hope to get
- Financial qualification was addressed naturally within the conversation

**Pass Examples:**
- "Have you been pre-approved for a mortgage?"
- "What price range are you comfortable with?"
- "Do you have a budget in mind?"
- "What are you hoping to get for the property?"
- "Are you a cash buyer or will you be financing?"

**Fail Examples:**
- Never asked about budget or financial readiness
- Call ended without understanding the caller's financial position

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 3: Identified Timeline (When Looking to Buy/Sell)

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent asked when the caller wants to buy or sell
- Agent determined urgency level
- A timeline was established (even if approximate)

**Pass Examples:**
- "What's your timeline for buying?"
- "When are you hoping to move?"
- "How soon do you need to sell?"
- "Is your lease ending soon?"
- "Are you actively looking or just starting to explore?"

**Fail Examples:**
- No discussion of when the caller wants to transact
- Timeline assumed but never confirmed

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 4: Discussed Specific Neighborhoods/Areas

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent asked about or discussed specific neighborhoods
- Agent demonstrated local knowledge of areas mentioned
- Conversation included neighborhood-specific information

**Pass Examples:**
- "What neighborhoods are you interested in?"
- "Howard Beach is great for families -- quiet streets, waterfront access."
- "Richmond Hill has a lot of two-family homes in your price range."
- "In South Ozone Park, the average home is selling for around..."
- Agent shared relevant neighborhood details when the caller mentioned an area

**Fail Examples:**
- Generic discussion without referencing specific neighborhoods
- Caller mentioned a neighborhood and agent had no response

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 5: Offered Value (CMA, Market Data, Showing, or Resource)

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent offered something concrete and valuable to the caller
- This could be: a CMA, comparable sales data, a showing, market information, a lender referral, or a property search setup
- The offer was specific, not vague

**Pass Examples:**
- "Let me run a free Comparative Market Analysis for you."
- "I'll set up a custom property search so you get new listings right away."
- "I have a great mortgage broker I can connect you with for pre-approval."
- "I can schedule a showing of three properties in that area this Saturday."
- "Let me pull up the recent sales in your neighborhood."

**Fail Examples:**
- No tangible offer made
- Vague: "I can help you" without specifying how
- Agent only answered questions without proactively offering anything

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 6: Handled Objections Professionally

**What AI Listens For:**
- If an objection was raised, agent addressed it calmly and constructively
- Agent acknowledged the concern before responding
- Response was factual, not defensive or dismissive
- If no objection was raised, this item is scored as Pass by default

**Common Objections Detected:**
- Commission questions
- "I'll use Zillow/Redfin"
- "Just browsing"
- "Prices are too high"
- "I have another agent"
- "Zillow says my home is worth..."
- "I want to try FSBO"
- "Discount broker charges less"

**Pass Examples:**
- "I completely understand that concern. Here's what I'd say..."
- "That's a great question. Let me explain how commission works..."
- "Zillow is a good starting point, but here's what it misses..."
- Agent remained calm, professional, and solution-oriented

**Fail Examples:**
- Agent became defensive or argumentative
- Agent dismissed the concern: "That's not true"
- Agent ignored the objection and changed the subject
- Agent couldn't articulate a response

**Scoring Note:** If no objection was raised during the call, this item scores as Pass (N/A counts as Pass).

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 7: Set Concrete Next Step (Appointment, Follow-Up, Showing)

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent proposed a specific next action with a date, time, or clear commitment
- The next step was agreed upon by both parties
- It was actionable, not vague

**Pass Examples:**
- "Let's schedule a showing for Thursday at 2 PM."
- "I'll call you Tuesday with the CMA results."
- "I'll email you five matching listings tonight."
- "Let's meet at the property Saturday at 10 AM."
- "I'll have the comparable sales data to you by tomorrow afternoon."

**Fail Examples:**
- "I'll be in touch." (vague, no timeframe)
- "Give me a call when you're ready." (puts burden on caller)
- Call ended without a next step
- "Let me know if you need anything." (no proactive commitment)

**Weight:** High -- this is the most important conversion indicator

---

### Item 8: Collected Contact Information

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent obtained at least a name and one contact method (phone or email)
- Agent confirmed the best way and time to follow up
- Contact info was verified (repeated back or confirmed)

**Pass Examples:**
- "Can I get your email so I can send you those listings?"
- "What's the best number to reach you at?"
- "Is this the best number, or do you prefer a different one?"
- "I have your name as [Name] and your email as [email] -- is that right?"
- Agent captured name + phone or name + email

**Fail Examples:**
- Call ended without getting any contact information
- Agent relied solely on caller ID without confirming
- Only first name captured with no way to follow up

**Weight:** High -- cannot follow up without contact info

---

### Item 9: Mentioned Unique Value Proposition

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent communicated at least one differentiator:
  - Community expertise (Indian, Guyanese, Bengali, Caribbean, Hispanic, Black communities)
  - Local/neighborhood knowledge (lives and works in the area)
  - Multilingual or culturally aware service
  - Online presence and marketing reach
  - Personal attention (solo agent, not a team pass-off)
  - Market expertise (years of experience, recent sales)

**Pass Examples:**
- "I work with a lot of families from the Indian community and I understand what's important."
- "I live right here in Ozone Park, so I know these streets inside and out."
- "My website has over 1,500 pages covering every neighborhood in Queens."
- "You'll work directly with me from start to finish -- I'm not going to hand you off to an assistant."
- "I specialize in two-family homes for buyers who want rental income."

**Fail Examples:**
- Purely transactional conversation with no differentiation
- Agent sounded like any generic agent -- no personal touch or local expertise shared
- Missed opportunities to connect on community or neighborhood knowledge

**Weight:** Standard

---

### Item 10: Proper Closing and Follow-Up Commitment

**What AI Listens For:**
- Agent closed the call professionally
- Agent stated what they will do next and when
- Agent thanked the caller
- Agent confirmed preferred communication method

**Pass Examples:**
- "Thank you for calling, [Name]. I'll send you those listings tonight and call you Thursday. Have a great day!"
- "I'm going to pull up the comps for your neighborhood right now and email them to you by end of day."
- "I'll text you a confirmation for our Saturday showing. Looking forward to meeting you!"
- "Great talking with you. I'll follow up Wednesday. Call or text me anytime at (917) 694-3993."

**Fail Examples:**
- "OK, bye." (abrupt, no commitment)
- Call ended without a clear goodbye
- No mention of what happens next
- Agent did not confirm how they will follow up

**Weight:** Standard

## Weekly Review Process

Every Monday morning:

1. Open Dialpad Analytics dashboard
2. Review average scorecard score for the past week
3. Identify the 3 most commonly missed items
4. Review 2-3 calls scored below 7/10
5. Listen to the call recordings and compare to AI scoring
6. Note patterns: Are objection handling or next-step setting consistently low?
7. Practice the weak areas using playbook scripts
8. Adjust scorecard criteria if any items are consistently irrelevant (e.g., objection handling on short info calls)
