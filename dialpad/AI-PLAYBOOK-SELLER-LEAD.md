# Dialpad AI Playbook: Inbound Seller Lead Call

**Playbook Name:** Seller Lead Qualification
**Trigger:** Auto-activate when caller mentions "sell," "list," "listing," "moving," "value," "worth," "what's my home worth," "market analysis," "CMA"
**Agent:** Nitin Gadura, Gadura Real Estate LLC
**Markets:** Queens, Long Island (Nassau County), Brooklyn

---

## Card 1: Professional Greeting

**Trigger:** Call begins
**Display Time:** First 30 seconds

### Script

> "Thank you for calling Gadura Real Estate, this is Nitin Gadura. How can I help you today?"

### Prompt Notes
- If they mention selling: "You've reached the right person -- I help homeowners in Queens and Long Island sell their properties every day. I'd love to learn more about your situation."
- If referral: "That's great -- [referrer name] is wonderful. I appreciate them connecting us."
- If from website: "Glad you found us online! Was it a particular page on the site that caught your eye, or did you fill out one of our home valuation forms?"

## Card 2: Property Details

**Trigger:** Caller expresses interest in selling
**Display Time:** 3-4 minutes

### Script

> "I'd love to learn about your property. Let me ask a few questions so I can give you the most accurate information. What's the address of the property?"

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask

**Address and Type:**
1. "What's the full address, including apartment number if applicable?"
2. "What type of property is it?"
   - Single-family house
   - Two-family house
   - Three-family / multi-family
   - Condo
   - Co-op
   - Townhouse
3. "How long have you owned the property?"

**Size and Layout:**
4. "How many bedrooms and bathrooms?"
5. "Do you know the approximate square footage?"
6. "How many floors/stories?"
7. "Does it have a basement? Is it finished?"
8. "Garage, driveway, or street parking?"
9. "Backyard -- what size roughly?"
10. "Any additional structures -- detached garage, shed, ADU?"

**For Two-Family or Multi-Family:**
11. "How many units?"
12. "Are all units currently occupied?"
13. "What are the current rents?"
14. "Are tenants on leases or month-to-month?"
15. "Are the tenants family or unrelated?"

**Condition and Updates:**
16. "On a scale of 1-10, how would you rate the overall condition?"
17. "Have you done any major renovations in the last 5-10 years?"
    - Kitchen
    - Bathrooms
    - Roof
    - Windows
    - Heating/HVAC
    - Electrical
    - Plumbing
    - Siding/exterior
18. "Is there anything that needs repair or updating right now?"
19. "Any known issues -- foundation, water, structural?"

**Unique Features:**
20. "What do you love most about the property? What would you highlight to a buyer?"

## Card 3: Seller Motivation and Timeline

**Trigger:** After property details captured
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script

> "Thanks for all that detail -- it sounds like a great property. Now, tell me a little about your situation. What's motivating you to consider selling?"

### Prompt Notes -- Questions to Ask

**Motivation:**
1. "What's the main reason you're thinking about selling?"
   - Upsizing (growing family)
   - Downsizing (empty nesters, retirement)
   - Relocating (job, family, lifestyle)
   - Financial reasons (divorce, debt, estate)
   - Investment liquidation
   - Inherited property
   - Tired landlord
2. "Have you already found your next home, or are you selling first?"
3. "Is anyone else involved in this decision -- spouse, partner, family members?"

**Timeline:**
4. "What's your ideal timeline for selling?"
   - "Ideally, when would you want to close?"
   - "Is there a date you need to be out by?"
   - "Are you flexible on timing, or is there urgency?"

**Previous Attempts:**
5. "Has the property been on the market before?"
   - If yes: "When was it listed, and why do you think it didn't sell?"
   - "Were you working with a specific agent?"
   - "What was the listing price?"

**Timeline Categories (internal notes):**
| Timeline | Lead Temperature | Follow-Up Priority |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| This month | Hot | Daily until listing appointment |
| 1-3 months | Warm | Every 3-4 days |
| 3-6 months | Nurture | Bi-weekly market updates |
| 6+ months or "someday" | Long-term | Monthly check-ins |

## Card 4: Price Expectations

**Trigger:** After motivation and timeline discussed
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script

> "Now, this is the question everyone wants to know -- do you have a price in mind for what you'd like to get for the property?"

### Prompt Notes

**If They Have a Number:**
- "That's helpful to know. Here's what I'd like to do -- I'll run a Comparative Market Analysis, which is a detailed report based on what similar properties in your neighborhood have actually sold for in the last 3-6 months. That'll give us a data-driven price that gets you the most money in the least time. Sometimes owners are pleasantly surprised, sometimes the market tells us something different, but either way you'll have the real numbers."

**If They Don't Have a Number:**
- "That's completely normal. That's exactly why I do a Comparative Market Analysis for every potential seller -- it takes the guesswork out of pricing. I'll research recent sales of similar properties in [neighborhood] and show you exactly what the market will pay. It's free, there's no obligation, and you'll walk away knowing what your home is worth. Can we set that up?"

**If Their Number Seems High:**
- Do NOT argue or correct them on the call
- "That's a great starting point. Let me run the numbers against recent comparable sales and I'll present everything to you. The data will tell us the story -- and I always want my clients to have the full picture so they can make the best decision."

**If They Mention Zillow Zestimate:**
- Trigger Card 7 (Objection: Zestimate)

## Card 5: CMA Offer and Listing Presentation

**Trigger:** After price discussion
**Display Time:** 2-3 minutes

### Script -- Present the CMA

> "Here's what I'd like to do as a next step, and this is completely free with no obligation. I'll put together a professional Comparative Market Analysis for your property. This includes:
>
> - Recent sold prices for similar homes in [neighborhood] in the last 3-6 months
> - Currently listed properties you'd be competing with
> - Market trends -- are prices going up, down, or flat in your area
> - A recommended listing price strategy that maximizes your sale price
> - An estimated net proceeds calculation so you know what you'll walk away with after closing costs, taxes, and commission
>
> I'll present all of this to you in person. It usually takes about 30-45 minutes, and you'll leave with a complete picture of your options. When would be a good time for me to come by?"

### Prompt Notes
- Always offer to do the CMA at the property: "I'd love to do this at your home so I can see the property in person -- my analysis is much more accurate when I can see the condition, layout, and updates firsthand."
- Propose specific times: "I have availability Thursday at 11 AM or Saturday at 2 PM -- which works better?"
- If they want it remotely: "I can absolutely do this over Zoom or phone if that's easier. I'll just need a few photos of the property to make the analysis as accurate as possible."
- Ask who should be present: "Will anyone else be involved in the decision to sell? I want to make sure everyone's questions get answered at the same time."

## Card 7: Objection Handling

**Trigger:** AI detects objection phrases
**Display Time:** Until resolved

### Objection: "I checked Zillow -- the Zestimate says my home is worth $X"

> "Zillow is a great starting point for a rough idea, but here's what a lot of homeowners don't realize -- Zillow's algorithm doesn't see inside your home. It doesn't know about your renovated kitchen, your finished basement, or the fact that you replaced the roof three years ago. And it doesn't know about that water damage in the neighbor's house that brought their sale price down, which Zillow then used to estimate yours.
>
> I've seen Zestimates be off by $50,000 to $100,000 or more in Queens -- sometimes higher, sometimes lower. My CMA uses actual closed sales of truly comparable properties, adjusted for your home's specific features and condition. It's the same methodology appraisers use. That's why I want to see your home in person and run the real numbers. No obligation -- you'll just have the facts."

### Objection: "I want to try For Sale By Owner first"

> "I completely respect that, and some homeowners do sell on their own successfully. Here's what I'd offer: even if you want to try FSBO, let me do the CMA for you at no cost. That way you have accurate pricing data to work with.
>
> Here's what I want you to consider, though: FSBO homes in our market typically sell for 10-15% less than agent-represented homes, according to the National Association of Realtors. And you'd be handling showings, negotiations, legal paperwork, inspections, and closing coordination on your own. A lot of FSBO sellers end up spending more time and netting less money than if they'd listed with an agent.
>
> My suggestion: try FSBO for 30 days with my CMA pricing. If it doesn't sell, let's talk again. I'd rather you feel confident in your decision either way. Fair enough?"

### Objection: "Another agent said they'd list it for less commission" / "Discount brokers charge only 1%"

> "I appreciate you doing your due diligence -- you absolutely should compare. Here's the question I'd ask: what are you getting for that commission?
>
> With a discount broker, you often get an MLS listing and a lockbox. With me, you get professional photography, a comprehensive marketing plan, my 1,500+ page web presence driving buyers to your listing, targeted marketing to diverse buyer communities, personal attention at every showing, expert negotiation, and full transaction management through closing.
>
> The difference in commission is usually a few thousand dollars. But the difference in sale price between a well-marketed home and an under-marketed one can be $20,000, $30,000, or more. My job is to net you the most money, not to cost you the least in fees. Let me show you the numbers in the CMA -- it'll be clear."

### Objection: "The market is bad / I should wait"

> "I understand the concern. Let me share what I'm actually seeing in [neighborhood] right now: [reference current market conditions -- inventory levels, days on market, buyer demand, interest rates].
>
> Here's the thing about timing the market -- it's nearly impossible, even for professionals. What we can control is how we price, present, and market your home. A well-priced, well-marketed home sells in any market.
>
> But I hear you -- if timing isn't right, it's not right. Let me run the CMA so you have current data, and then you can decide when to pull the trigger. You'll be making a decision based on facts, not feelings. Sound fair?"

### Objection: "I need to talk to my spouse/family first"

> "Absolutely -- this is a big decision and everyone involved should be on the same page. Here's what I'd suggest: let me schedule the CMA presentation at a time when both of you can be there. That way you both hear the same information, you can ask questions together, and you can discuss it privately afterward with all the facts. When would work for both of you?"

### Objection: "I'm interviewing multiple agents"

> "Smart move -- you should. I'd be disappointed if you didn't do your homework. All I'd ask is that you include me in the interview. When I do my listing presentation, I'll show you the CMA, my marketing plan, and how I've performed for other sellers in [neighborhood]. After that, you'll have everything you need to make the best choice. When can I come by?"

### Within 24 Hours
- [ ] Run preliminary CMA research on the property
- [ ] Send a brief email with 2-3 comparable sales as a teaser
- [ ] Calendar the CMA presentation appointment
- [ ] Research the property on public records (lot size, tax assessment, deed history)

### Pre-CMA Appointment Preparation
- [ ] Full Comparative Market Analysis with 6-8 comparable sales
- [ ] Net proceeds estimate at 3 price points (below, at, and above estimated market value)
- [ ] Written marketing plan customized to the property
- [ ] Pre-listing checklist for the homeowner (staging tips, repairs to consider)
- [ ] Listing agreement (pre-filled with property details)
- [ ] Professional presentation folder or digital presentation

### Follow-Up Cadence
| Lead Temperature | First Follow-Up | Cadence |
|-----------------|----------------|---------|
| Hot (CMA appointment set) | Same day confirmation | Check in 2 days before appointment |
| Warm (interested, no appointment) | 2-3 days with comps | Weekly for 4 weeks |
| Cool (exploring options) | 1 week with market update | Bi-weekly for 8 weeks |
| Long-term (not ready) | 2 weeks | Monthly market updates + quarterly personal call |
